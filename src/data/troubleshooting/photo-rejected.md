Your **Vietnam eVisa photo was rejected**—or your application is stuck because immigration flagged the upload. This is one of the most fixable visa problems if you act quickly: retake the portrait, resize correctly, and resubmit before your processing window expires.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** Photo standards follow official eVisa rules. We help you format compliant files before submission.

## Quick answer: rejected photo fixes

| Rejection cause | Fast fix |
| --- | --- |
| Patterned or coloured background | Plain white sheet behind you; stand ~1 m away |
| Face too small in frame | Crop tighter—full head and slight shoulders |
| Blurry or dark image | Daylight near a window; hold phone steady |
| Glasses glare | Retake without glasses or tilt to remove reflection |
| Beauty filter / heavy editing | Turn off all phone filters; use natural skin tone |
| Wrong file uploaded | Separate **portrait** from **passport bio-page scan** |
| File too large or wrong dimensions | Resize to portal pixels; JPEG ~85% quality |

For full specifications, see [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements 2026](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026).

## Portrait photo vs passport scan (do not mix them up)

Most rejections come from uploading the wrong file type:

| Upload slot | Submit this |
| --- | --- |
| **Portrait / personal photo** | Recent studio-style headshot—not the photo printed inside your passport |
| **Passport scan** | Full colour bio page with MRZ lines, holograms, all four corners |

Swapping these files is an automatic fail on many portals.

## Retake your portrait in five minutes

1. Hang a **white or off-white** sheet on a wall
2. Stand facing the camera at eye level—no selfie angle from below
3. Use **even daylight**; avoid one-sided shadows
4. **No filters**, hats (unless religious covering with full face visible), or sunglasses
5. Export to the **exact pixel size** shown on the upload screen
6. Compare your face to your passport photo—if you grew a beard or changed hair dramatically, immigration may flag a mismatch

### Glasses and head coverings

- **Prescription glasses:** sometimes accepted if lenses are clear and glare-free; when in doubt, retake without
- **Religious head coverings:** permitted when the full face is visible from hairline to chin

### Children and infants

Babies need their own compliant photo—parents cannot use a selfie with the child. See [/faq/children-visa-vietnam](/faq/children-visa-vietnam) for infant framing tips.

## Fix your passport bio-page scan

- Lay passport flat on a dark surface
- Photograph straight-on—not at an angle
- All four corners visible; no fingers covering data
- MRZ lines (bottom two rows of chevrons) must be sharp and readable
- Use colour—not black-and-white filter

Blurry MRZ scans cause delays even when the portrait is perfect.

## After rejection: resubmit vs new application

### Immigration sent a resubmission link

- Fix the specific issue noted in the email
- Upload within the stated deadline—pending clocks may pause until you respond
- Keep the same reference number

### Full rejection with no correction path

- Prepare new portrait and passport scan before paying again
- Double-check name and passport number against MRZ on [/apply](/apply)
- Select **Urgent** or **Super Urgent** if your flight is soon

Contact support before paying twice—see [stuck processing guide](/troubleshooting/stuck-processing).

## Indian and high-volume applicants: extra photo tips

Indian passport holders face high application volume—photo quality scrutiny is strict. Common failures:

- Selfies with ceiling fans or furniture in background
- Passport scan cropped too tight on MRZ
- Name on form not matching passport romanization
