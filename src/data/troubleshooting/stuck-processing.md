Your **Vietnam eVisa is still pending**—three days, five days, maybe longer—and your flight is approaching. Your status page shows "Processing" or "Unfulfilled," your inbox is empty, and panic sets in. This guide explains what "pending" actually means, when delay is normal, and exactly what to do before you pay for a duplicate application.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** Final approval timing is decided by Vietnamese immigration authorities. We can help track your filing, fix document issues, and escalate when your travel date is at risk.

## Quick answer: Vietnam eVisa pending timelines

| Tier (our service) | Target window | Action if exceeded |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Normal** | Within **5 business days** | Contact support after 5 business days without outcome |
| **Urgent** | **3 days** | Escalate if day 3 passes with no email |
| **Super Urgent** | **1 day** | Contact support same day if no approval |
| **Weekends / Tet** | Slower review | Add 2–4+ extra calendar days—see [weekend guide](/troubleshooting/weekend-processing) |

**Business days** exclude Saturdays, Sundays, and Vietnamese public holidays. A Friday submission may not count Saturday or Sunday toward your window.

For full tier explanations, read [Vietnam eVisa processing time 2026](/blog/vietnam-evisa-processing-time-2026).

## Why your eVisa may still show "pending"

### Normal immigration backlog

High-volume periods—December–January holidays, Tet (Lunar New Year), April reunification holidays, and summer peak—stretch review queues. "Pending" often means your file is in line, not lost.

### Document quality hold

Blurry passport scans, non-compliant photos, or mismatched portrait vs bio page can push your file into manual review. Check email for a **resubmission request** before assuming silence means approval is coming.

### Payment not fully captured

Sometimes the form submits but payment fails or times out. Verify your bank statement for a successful charge. Without confirmed payment, the application may never enter the review queue.

### Email typo on the application

Approval emails go to the address typed on the form. A single wrong character sends your PDF elsewhere while you wait on the correct inbox. Search every folder and confirm the email address on your receipt.

### Security or eligibility screening

Some applications receive extended review with minimal public explanation. Duplicate filings, mismatched travel history, or data anomalies can add days. Contact support rather than guessing.

## What to do when pending exceeds 5 days

### Step 1: Audit your inbox and portal

- Search: your reference number, "eVisa," "immigration," "Vietnam"
- Check spam, promotions, and "Updates" tabs (Gmail)
- Log into any status-check tool with **exact surname spelling** from your passport

### Step 2: Verify payment

- Confirm charge on card or bank statement
- Match amount to your selected tier on [/fees](/fees)
- Save receipt screenshot for support

### Step 3: Contact support—do not duplicate yet

Provide:

- Reference number
- Full name as on passport
- Application date and tier selected
- Travel date and flight number
- Screenshots of portal status

**Avoid paying twice.** Duplicate applications are a top cause of extended fraud holds. See [common eVisa mistakes](/blog/vietnam-evisa-common-mistakes-2026).

### Step 4: Fix document issues if requested

If immigration asked for a new photo or clearer scan, resubmit **immediately**. Pending clocks often pause until you respond.

For photo problems, see [Vietnam eVisa photo rejected](/troubleshooting/photo-rejected).

### Step 5: Escalate urgency if travel is imminent

When your flight is within **72 hours**:

- Request Super Urgent handling if still eligible
- Ask whether a corrected re-filing is faster than waiting on the current file
- Prepare to reschedule if approval does not arrive before online check-in closes

## "Unfulfilled" vs "Processing" vs "Rejected"

| Status | Likely meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Processing / Pending** | In queue or awaiting document fix |
| **Unfulfilled** | Payment or required step incomplete—or portal lag; verify with support |
| **Rejected** | Decision issued—read email for reason; usually requires new application |
| **Approved** | PDF attached—download and verify all fields |

If status says rejected but you never received email, contact support with your reference number before re-applying.

## High-volume nationalities and realistic expectations

Indian, Chinese, American, and UK passport holders file in large numbers during peak season. Individual timelines vary even within the same tier. Your friend's two-day approval does not guarantee yours—document quality and review depth differ.

Check nationality-specific tips on [/check-requirement/india](/check-requirement/india) and your country page before escalating.

## When waiting is still the right move

- You are within your tier window on **business days**
- Payment confirmed and email address correct
- No document resubmission request outstanding
- No duplicate application filed elsewhere

If all above are true, daily status refresh is enough—escalate when the tier window passes.

## When to re-apply (only with guidance)

Re-application may be appropriate when:

- Support confirms the original filing is void or unrecoverable
- Rejection email received with no correction path
- Wrong passport number or name on an approved PDF (see [wrong name fix](/troubleshooting/wrong-name-evisa))

Always get support confirmation before a second payment.
