## Wrong airport or border on your Vietnam eVisa? This mistake is more common than you think

You booked flights to **Da Nang (DAD)** but the form defaulted to **Tan Son Nhat (SGN)**. Or you planned a **land crossing from Cambodia** but selected an airport you will never use. The rest of your application can be perfect—name, passport, dates—and you can still be stopped because **where you enter matters**.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** We help travelers align itineraries with compliant applications; we cannot re-route you through a port not on your approval.

## Why port of entry is not a minor detail

Your eVisa authorizes entry at **specific designated ports**. Immigration systems and airlines treat the port list as binding for eVisa holders—not as a preference you can change at arrival.

## The harsh truth: wrong port = wrong trip

### Airlines can refuse boarding before you leave home

Carriers check that your eVisa ports align with your inbound flight to Vietnam. A ticket to Da Nang with an eVisa listing only Ho Chi Minh City is a classic denial scenario.

### Border officers can refuse entry even if you flew anyway

If you reach Vietnam at an unlisted port, you may be denied entry, held for return flights, or face penalties—**after** you have already spent money on transport.

### Changing plans at the airport does not rewrite your eVisa

Buying a new domestic ticket inside Vietnam does not fix an international arrival mismatch. The first Vietnamese port of entry must match.

## Common situations we see

### Connecting flights through a third country

Your **first landing in Vietnam** controls the port—not where you started (e.g., London or Bangkok).

### Multi-city tours (Hanoi in, Ho Chi Minh out)

Some itineraries need multiple-entry visas or careful port selection. Single-entry eVisas still require the **first** entry port to be listed correctly.

### Land border vs airport confusion

Moc Bai, Lao Bao, and other gates are not interchangeable with airports. Select the exact border or airport on your route.

## Your options (pick one before you fly)

### Option A: Change your flight to the approved port

Sometimes the fastest fix is flying into the city already on your eVisa—if fares and schedules still work.

### Option B: Obtain an eVisa listing your real arrival port

If flights are locked, plan a corrected application path. Treat the wrong approval as invalid for travel.

### Option C: Confirm whether your approval already lists multiple ports

Read the full port list—occasionally more than one gate is authorized. Do not assume; verify character by character.
