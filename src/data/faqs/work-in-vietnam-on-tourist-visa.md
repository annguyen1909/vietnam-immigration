## Is It Allowed to Work in Vietnam While on a Tourist eVisa?

**No, you cannot work in Vietnam on a tourist eVisa.** Any form of employment—paid or unpaid, including remote work, freelancing, or digital nomad activities—is strictly prohibited under Vietnamese immigration law if you are on a tourist eVisa. Violating these rules can result in serious legal consequences, including fines, deportation, and future travel bans.

## What Activities Are Allowed on a Tourist eVisa?

A Vietnam tourist eVisa is only valid for:

- Sightseeing and holidays
- Visiting friends and relatives
- Medical treatment or wellness retreats
- Participating in short-term cultural or religious events (as a visitor)

**You may NOT:**

- Take up any form of employment (full-time, part-time, or freelance)
- Volunteer or intern (even unpaid)
- Conduct business or commercial activities

## Consequences of Working Illegally in Vietnam

If you are caught working on a tourist eVisa, you may face:

- **Fines and Penalties:** Heavy fines for overstaying or violating visa conditions
- **Deportation:** Immediate removal from Vietnam and possible detention
- **Future Travel Restrictions:** A ban on re-entering Vietnam or other countries
- **Employer Risks:** Companies hiring foreigners without proper permits can also be fined or prosecuted

**Important:** Vietnamese authorities take visa violations seriously. Immigration checks are common, especially in tourist and business hubs.

## How to Legally Work in Vietnam

To work in Vietnam, you must obtain the correct visa **before** entering the country:

### Work Permit

- For formal employment with a registered Vietnamese company
- Requires sponsorship from the employer
- Must be obtained before entering Vietnam

### Business Visa

- For business meetings, conferences, or short-term business activities
- Different from tourist visa
- Contact Vietnamese embassy for requirements
