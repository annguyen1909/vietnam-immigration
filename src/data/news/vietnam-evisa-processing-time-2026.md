**How long does a Vietnam eVisa take?** On assisted flows, **Normal** service is commonly **within five business days** after payment; **Urgent (3 days)** and **Super Urgent (1 day)** tiers are optional when your arrival date allows. Government approval still depends on immigration workload, Vietnamese public holidays, document quality, and security screening—so "processing time" is a planning range, not a boarding guarantee.

This 2026 guide explains realistic timelines, what speeds or slows review, how urgent tiers actually work, and what to do when your flight is closer than your approval email.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** We can prepare and submit your application quickly; final approval timing is decided by Vietnamese immigration authorities. No private service can guarantee approval by a specific date or flight.

## Standard processing: what happens after you pay

After you submit and pay on [/apply](/apply), your package enters the immigration review pipeline handled by [Vietnamese immigration authorities](https://immigration.gov.vn/). On the assisted side, **Normal** handling targets approval **within five business days** after payment—when document quality is high and volume is normal.

**Typical sequence:**

1. **Payment confirmation** and reference number issued
2. **Document and data review**—automated checks plus human review when needed
3. **Email outcome**—approval PDF, or a request to fix uploads or data
4. **Your action**—download, verify fields, print before check-in

**Important distinction:** Completing the online form in 20 minutes is not the same as receiving immigration approval. Processing clock starts after successful payment and submission—not when you begin typing.

## What makes Vietnam eVisa processing faster

High-quality applications clear automated checks quickly and rarely bounce back for corrections.

**Speed factors you control:**

- **Sharp passport scan** with fully readable MRZ lines—see [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026)
- **Passport-style portrait** on plain background—no selfies or filters
- **MRZ-perfect data**—passport number, name, date of birth matching bio page exactly
- **Correct nationality** and visa purpose selection
- **Consistent group data** for families—[Vietnam eVisa for families and minors](/blog/vietnam-evisa-for-minors-families-2026)
- **Applying early** in the day on a weekday with complete documents ready

**Planning advice:** Upload files once correctly rather than cycling three marginal versions that each reset review time.

## What slows Vietnam eVisa processing down

Even perfect applications wait longer during peak periods. Others slow because of fixable errors.

### Document and data delays

- Blurry uploads, wrong file types, or swapped portrait vs passport files
- Typos in name, passport number, or date of birth triggering manual rejection loops
- Infant photos that fail automated face detection

Fix root causes before reapplying—see [Vietnam eVisa rejection reasons](/blog/vietnam-evisa-rejection-reasons-2026) and [common mistakes](/blog/vietnam-evisa-common-mistakes-2026).

### Volume and holiday backlogs

Human review slows when application volume spikes:

| Period | Typical impact |
| --- | --- |
| **Tet (Lunar New Year)** | Significant slowdown; apply weeks early |
| **December–January** | High tourist volume |
| **April holidays** | Domestic travel peaks |
| **June–August** | Summer family travel surge |

Portals may remain **online during weekends and holidays**, but approval emails still depend on when officers process the queue.

### Security and duplicate-application holds

- **Duplicate paid applications** for the same passport
- Database flags on **passport reports** or travel history
- Multiple rapid card attempts from unusual IP ranges

**Do not** submit a second paid application because the first "seems slow." Contact support with your reference number first.

### Technical and email issues

- Payment succeeded but status stuck—reference email missing
- **Email typo** on the form—approval sent elsewhere while you wait
- Approval sitting in **spam or promotions** folder

Search all inbox folders before assuming processing failed.

## Weekends, business days, and Vietnamese holidays

"Five business days" means **working days**, not calendar days—including on the government side. A Friday evening submission may not enter active review until the next business week.

**Practical planning:**

- Submit **Monday–Wednesday** when possible for Normal tier margin
- Avoid assuming **same-day approval** on weekends even with Super Urgent selected
- Around **Tet**, add **1–2 weeks** of extra margin beyond your tier label

This is planning guidance. Only your approval email confirms completion.

## Tracking your Vietnam eVisa application status

Keep these items accessible from payment through travel day:

- **Application reference number**
- **Email address** exactly as typed on the form
- **Payment confirmation** (card receipt or processor email)
- **Passport number** submitted (for support lookups)

If a portal offers status lookup, use the **same surname spelling** as on your passport. Typos in lookup fields make valid applications appear missing.

**When to contact support:**

- Processing exceeds your **selected tier window**
- Payment charged but **no reference email** within a few hours
- Correction request received but unclear which document failed
- Family group where **some passengers approved** and others still pending

## If approval has not arrived: step-by-step

1. **Search spam, junk, and promotions** in every inbox folder
2. **Verify payment succeeded**—one charge, one reference number
3. **Confirm passport validity**—six months beyond arrival; correct passport booklet
4. **Review uploads**—would you approve the scan if you were an officer?
5. **Contact support** with reference number and payment proof
6. **Do not** submit duplicate paid applications unless support advises
7. **Delay non-refundable bookings** until PDF arrives
8. **Consider tier upgrade or reapplication** only with guidance if rejection occurred

## When to choose urgent or super urgent tiers

**Choose Urgent (3 days)** when:

- Departure is **4–7 days** away
- Documents are high quality and unlikely to need rework
- You cannot move travel dates easily

**Choose Super Urgent (1 day)** when:

- Departure is **2–3 days** away
- You understand approval is still not guaranteed by immigration
- You have contacted support if filing within 72 hours

**Stick with Normal** when:

- Departure is **14+ days** away
- You are still gathering child photos or passport renewals
- You want **standard pricing** and can wait

Confirm eligibility for each tier at checkout—tiers may be unavailable if your stated arrival date is too soon for the system to accept them.

## After approval: timing your check-in and arrival

Processing ends when you hold the PDF—not when email arrives while you are asleep. Before heading to the airport:

- Download and **print** the PDF
- Verify **passport number, name, dates, entry port, entry type**
- Save offline copy on phone
- For families, confirm **every passenger's** PDF

Airlines may deny boarding if approval cannot be verified in their system—even minutes after you receive email.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does Vietnam eVisa approval take in 2026?

Normal handling is commonly **within five business days** after payment on assisted flows. Urgent and Super Urgent tiers target shorter windows when your arrival date allows. Holidays and document issues extend timelines.

### Is same-day Vietnam eVisa processing possible?

Sometimes when volume is low and documents are perfect—but never rely on it for tight itineraries. Government timing is not contractually same-day.

### Does urgent fee guarantee approval before my flight?

No. Rush fees speed preparation and submission on the assisted side. Immigration authorities control final approval.

### How early should I apply before travel?

**7–14+ days** when possible. Families and holiday travel should aim for the longer end.

### Do weekends count toward processing time?

Business-day targets exclude weekends and public holidays for realistic planning—even if portals stay online.

### Why is my eVisa still processing after 5 days?

Volume, holidays, document correction loops, security screening, or email delivery issues. Contact support with reference number before duplicating payment.

### Can I upgrade to urgent after I already paid?

Contact support with your reference number immediately. Options depend on current status and arrival date.

### What if I fly before approval arrives?

Do not board without valid authorization. Contact support, review emergency guides, and delay non-refundable plans until you hold the PDF or another eligible visa.
