**How long does a Vietnam eVisa take?** **Normal** service is **within 5 business days**; **Urgent (3 days)** and **Super Urgent (1 day)** are optional when you apply. Government approval still depends on volume, holidays, and document quality—plan ahead.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** We can help prepare and submit your package quickly; final approval timing is decided by Vietnamese immigration authorities.

> **Flight Leaving Soon?** If you've been waiting longer than your selected tier allows and your visa is still in "Processing" status, read our [Emergency Guide: eVisa Stuck Processing](/troubleshooting/stuck-processing) to get it expedited.

## Standard processing window

After you submit and pay on [/apply](/apply):

1. Authorities review passport data and uploads.
2. Email arrives with **approval** or a request to fix issues.
3. You download and print the PDF before airline check-in.

The website may stay online during **weekends and Vietnamese public holidays** (especially **Tet**), but human review can still slow down.

## What makes processing faster

- Sharp passport scan and compliant portrait ([photo guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026)).
- Passport number, name, and dates matching the **MRZ** exactly.
- Correct nationality and **entry port** for your real route.
- Applying **several days** before travel—not at the airport gate.

## What slows processing down

- Blurry uploads, wrong file types, or swapped portrait vs passport files.
- Typos in name, passport number, or date of birth.
- High volume: December–January, **Tet**, April holidays, summer peaks.
- Repeated resubmissions after rejection without fixing root cause.

Within **48 hours** of flying? Read [/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa](/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa) and contact support immediately.

## Urgent or “rush” services explained

Assisted services may offer **Urgent (3 days)** (+$110/pax) or **Super Urgent (1 day)** (+$220/pax) on our portal—checking photos, catching typos, and submitting promptly. **Government approval time** still depends on immigration workload. Paying for faster handling does not bypass legal screening.

Compare service levels on [/fees](/fees) and our processing page at `/processing` if your site lists tiers.

## Tracking your application status

Keep your **reference number** and the email used on the form. If a portal offers status lookup, use the same surname spelling as on the passport. Save payment confirmation in case support needs it.

## If approval has not arrived

1. Check **spam/junk** folders.
2. Verify **payment succeeded** (no duplicate charges without a reference).
3. Confirm passport **six-month validity** beyond arrival.
4. Avoid **non-refundable hotels** until you hold the PDF.
5. Contact support with reference number—**do not** submit duplicate paid applications unless advised.

## Nationality and document scrutiny

Timelines are similar for many countries, but review depth varies. Confirm rules on [/check-requirement](/check-requirement) before assuming a friend’s timeline applies to your passport.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does approval take?**  
Often hours to a few working days after payment—not guaranteed by your flight date.

**Is same-day possible?**  
Sometimes, when quality is high and volume is low—never rely on it for tight itineraries.

**Does urgent fee guarantee approval?**  
No—it speeds preparation and submission, not government decisions.

**What if I fly before approval?**  
Contact support; do not board without valid visa unless you have another eligible entry document.
