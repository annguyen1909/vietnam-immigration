## How Vietnamese markets differ by city

Not all "markets" serve the same shopper:

- **Tourist halls** (Ben Thanh, Hoi An riverside) optimise for visitors—higher opening quotes, English labels, wrap services. Use them to **learn price bands**, not always to find the lowest wholesale rate.
- **Wholesale floors** (Dong Xuan downstairs, HCMC fabric districts) skew local—bulk candy, notions, winter wear before **Sa Pa** trips. Less souvenir packaging, better fabric metre prices if you sew.
- **Ethnic weekend bazaars** (Bac Ha, Can Cau) are cultural events as much as shops—livestock, herbs, indigo cloth. Arrive early; photography requires consent.
- **Night markets** trade heat for atmosphere—lantern glow, grilled seafood, tailoring rush orders. Quality varies; inspect stitching under light.

**Regional comparison:** **Ho Chi Minh City** markets push coffee, pepper, and lacquer. **Hanoi** markets lean propaganda prints, silk, and cold-weather gear. **Hoi An** specialises lanterns, tailor shops, and leather sandals. Plan purchases where authenticity and repair access are strongest—re-stitch a Hoi An suit locally; ship coffee from either big city.

## Ben Thanh Market, Ho Chi Minh City

**Ben Thanh** is the default **Ho Chi Minh City market** for first visits—apparel, dried fruit, **Dak Lak robusta**, **Saigon cinnamon**, pepper, and lacquerware under one roof. Midday interior beats perimeter pavement heat; evening perimeter stalls skew street food—watch bags after **18:00**.

### Smart buys and price benchmarks

| Item | Opening stall band (VND) | Quality check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T-shirt | 120,000–250,000 | Seam straightness, dye smell |
| Coffee 250 g (labelled) | 150,000–350,000 | Origin label, roast date if printed |
| Lacquer plate (small) | 300,000–800,000 | Even glaze, no bubble pits |
| Dried mango / jackfruit | 40,000–80,000 per 100 g | Vacuum seal, ingredient list |
| Cinnamon sticks | 50,000–120,000 per 100 g | Aroma strength, brittle snap |

**Benchmark trick:** Walk to a nearby **supermarket shelf** (VinMart, Co.opmart) before haggling—knowing retail coffee price prevents absurd "discounts" from inflated opens.

**Bargaining script (polite):** Smile, ask total, offer **10–15% below** if you are buying multiple items, bundle politely. If the vendor laughs and holds firm, pay or walk—do not perform outrage.

**Common mistake:** Shopping Ben Thanh on your last hour before **Tan Son Nhat (SGN)** flight—wrap and weight eat time. Shop mid-trip; hotel midday drop strategy below.

## Dong Xuan Market, Hanoi

**Dong Xuan** skews **wholesale** downstairs—fabric rolls, notions, bulk candy—while fringe alleys sell **propaganda prints**, scarves, and winter wear before northern mountain trips.

### Dong Xuan shopping tactics

- Compare **three stalls** before committing on fabric or prints.
- Lift ceramics to check glaze consistency—hairline cracks fail shipping.
- Bubble wrap often **15,000–50,000 VND**—patient vendors help; tip if they double-box.
- **Old Quarter** shops nearby sell fixed-price lacquer if haggling fatigues you.

**Best time:** Weekday mornings **08:00–11:00**—less motorbike chaos at loading bays. Weekends crowd wholesale buyers.

**Pair with:** **Hang Gai** (silk street) and **Hang Bac** (silver) for specialised browsing—Dong Xuan is volume, not always finest silk.

## Hoi An Night Market and tailoring zone

**Nguyen Hoang** lanes fill with **lantern stalls**, coconut-leaf crafts, grilled seafood aromas, and leather sandal shops. Hoi An is Vietnam's **tailoring capital**—rush orders possible but risky.

### Tailoring and leather: what to verify

| Factor | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| **Fitting schedule** | Allow two fittings minimum; "overnight suit" promises often skip structure |
| **Seam allowance** | Cheaper shops omit ease—jackets pull across shoulders |
| **Fabric source** | Ask if wool is tropical-weight; synthetics suit humidity |
| **Leather sandals** | Inspect stitching under phone flashlight; glue-only soles fail in rain |
| **Deposit terms** | 50% deposits are common—get written pickup time |

**Lantern buys:** Collapsible silk frames pack better than rigid bamboo. Hand-painted panels cost more—confirm bulb socket type (EU vs US adapters).

**Bargaining:** Polite after you intend to buy. Respect **no-photo** signs on living vendors.

**Route logic:** Base in **Hoi An Ancient Town**; day-trip **Da Nang** malls for benchmarks. Fly into **Da Nang (DAD)** if Hoi An is your first Vietnam stop—confirm [Vietnam eVisa entry ports](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026).

## Regional and ethnic markets worth timing

Beyond the big three halls:

- **Bac Ha Sunday market** (Lao Cai): Flower Hmong textiles, livestock colour—arrive Saturday night. See our [hidden gems in northern Vietnam](/blog/hidden-gems-vietnam-2026) guide for loop logic.
- **Can Cau** (Saturday) and **Coc Ly** (Tuesday): Smaller Bac Ha alternatives.
- **Sa Pa weekend stalls:** Trekking gear and hill-tribe textiles—higher tourist markups than Bac Ha; compare embroidery quality.
- **Da Lat night market:** Strawberries, artichoke tea, wool wear—cool-climate souvenirs unlike lowland stalls.

Ethnic markets reward **cash in small notes** and **portrait permission**. Livestock trading is livelihood, not backdrop—step aside for animal handlers.

### Avoid or treat with caution

- **"Weasel coffee" tubs** without traceable origin—often marketing fiction.
- **Coral, turtle shell, ivory, pangolin products**—illegal or restricted imports in most countries.
- **Replica luxury watches and sportswear**—assume fake at market prices.
- **Unlabelled herbal powders**—customs and allergies both object.

## Bargaining in Vietnam: etiquette and strategy

Bargaining is **not** combat—it is rapport. Rules that protect both sides:

1. **Browse without haggling** until you intend to buy—opening every item's price wastes vendor time.
2. **Use supermarket benchmarks**—know coffee's shelf price before negotiating "50% off."
3. **Bundle politely**—"If I take two scarves, can you include wrap?" beats aggressive halving.
4. **Walk away calmly**—sometimes they call you back; sometimes they do not. Both outcomes are fine.
5. **Fixed-price shops exist**—especially Hoi An boutiques. Respect posted prices.
6. **Tip wrap help** if extraordinary—**10,000–20,000 VND** is appreciated.

**Fatigue tax:** On hour four, you overpay. Use a **photo price note** on your phone; take a café break before big purchases.

## VAT refunds and international shipping

Participating retailers issue **VAT refund forms** when purchases exceed current minimum thresholds. At **SGN** or **HAN** airport, present goods, receipts, and passport at refund counters **before** check-in—allow extra time.

**Shipping home:** Use honest customs declarations for crafts and wood products. Couriers ask contents—"decorative lacquer bowl" beats vague "gift." Insurance worth it for ceramics.

Official refund rules change; confirm at point of sale whether the shop participates—this is **government programme policy**, not something our visa service administers.

## Frequently asked questions

### What should I buy as souvenirs in Vietnam?

Labelled coffee, tea, silk from reputable shops, Hoi An lanterns, lacquerware, and cinnamon. Avoid wildlife products and opaque coffee claims.

### Is bargaining expected in Vietnamese markets?

Yes in tourist halls, once you intend to buy. Use supermarket benchmarks; stay polite; walk away if needed.

### Do markets accept credit cards?

Most stalls are cash-only. Malls accept cards—use them for price comparison, not only convenience.

### Can I claim VAT refunds?

At participating retailers with eligible spend—present goods and receipts at airport counters before departure. Rules are set by tax authorities.

### Which market is best for first-time visitors?

Ben Thanh (HCMC) and Dong Xuan (Hanoi) for variety. Hoi An for lanterns and tailoring.

### How much cash for a market day?

500,000–2,000,000 VND light; 3,000,000–5,000,000 VND for serious craft days. Carry smaller notes.

### Are counterfeit goods common?

Assume replicas for branded apparel and watches at stall prices. Buy authenticity from authorised stores.

### Do I need a Vietnam eVisa before shopping trips?

Most visitors do. Match entry airport to your PDF port before booking flights.
