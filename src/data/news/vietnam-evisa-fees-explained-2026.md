Understanding each layer before you pay prevents surprise charges at checkout and helps you compare assisted filing against the official government-only route.

This guide breaks down every fee category, shows realistic totals for solo travelers and families, explains what is refundable, and flags the hidden costs travelers forget until they are already at the airport.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** Vietnamese immigration authorities set mandatory government fees and make approval decisions. We disclose every fee line before checkout so you know the full total before authorizing payment. You may also file directly through official government channels at government fee only.

## Government fees: the four standard visa types

All standard online products on this site authorize up to a **90-day stay**. Under current published pricing, **government fees depend on visa type—not nationality**. A US passport holder and a Brazilian passport holder pay the same government fee for the same product.

| Visa type | Government fee (USD) | Entry type | Typical purpose |
| :--- | ---: | :--- | :--- |
| Tourist eVisa (Single Entry, 90 days) | **$55** | One arrival period | Holidays, family visits, sightseeing |
| Tourist eVisa (Multiple Entries, 90 days) | **$80** | Leave and re-enter within validity | Southeast Asia loops, side trips |
| Business eVisa (Single Entry, 90 days) | **$55** | One arrival period | Meetings, conferences, short commercial visits |
| Business eVisa (Multiple Entries, 90 days) | **$80** | Leave and re-enter within validity | Recurring business travel in the region |

These amounts are **mandatory government charges** for the visa authorization itself. They are the same whether you file through the official government portal or through a private assistance service—the difference is what additional fees appear on top.

### Tourism vs business: same price, different rules

Government fees are identical for tourism and business at the same entry type. What changes is how you must behave in Vietnam:

- **Tourist eVisa** covers holidays, family visits, and leisure travel. It does **not** authorize local employment or paid work for Vietnamese clients.
- **Business eVisa** covers meetings, factory visits, conferences, and short commercial trips without local payroll.

Selecting the wrong purpose category can create problems at immigration or with employers—not a different fee tier. If your trip mixes tourism and light business meetings, choose honestly and carry supporting documents if asked.

### Why nationality does not change the government fee

Some travelers assume visa costs vary by passport because embassy sticker visas sometimes do. On our eVisa portal, **eligibility** may differ by nationality (confirm on [Vietnam visa eligibility by country](/check-requirement)), but the **published government fee** for standard 90-day products stays at $55 or $80.

Always verify live pricing at [/fees](/fees). Forum posts from prior years or screenshots from other websites are unreliable—fee schedules and product names change.

## Assisted service fee: what $59.99 per passenger covers

Private assistance on our portal adds **$59.99 per passenger**. This is **separate from and in addition to** the government fee. It pays for:

- Guided form completion with field validation
- Document format checks (passport scan quality, photo dimensions, MRZ readability)
- Customer support during processing and correction requests
- Email follow-up if authorities request resubmission
- Fee transparency at every checkout step

The service fee does **not** replace the government charge, guarantee approval, or bypass immigration security review. Both assisted and official paths submit to the same government decision process.

### Official government portal vs assisted filing: cost comparison

| Path | What you pay | What you get |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Official government eVisa portal** | Government fee only ($55 or $80) | Direct filing; you handle uploads, fields, and corrections yourself |
| **Assisted filing (this site)** | Government fee + $59.99/pax service fee | Validation, support, disclosed totals, document review before submission |

Assisted filing tends to help travelers who want a structured checklist, are filing for families, need multiple-entry confirmation, or worry about passport scan and photo rejections. If you are comfortable self-filing and cost is the priority, the official route is legitimate—just budget time for careful data entry.

Neither path can override a government rejection. What assistance can reduce is **avoidable errors**—typos in passport numbers, wrong date-of-birth format, blurry scans—that cause delays or refusals.

## Optional urgent processing: Urgent and Super Urgent tiers

**Critical distinction:** Rush fees speed **application preparation and submission** on the assisted side. They do **not** guarantee government approval by a specific flight time. Immigration review still depends on workload, Vietnamese public holidays (especially **Tet**), document quality, and security screening.

Never book non-refundable flights until you hold the approved PDF. Realistic planning windows: [Vietnam eVisa processing time](/blog/vietnam-evisa-processing-time-2026).

### When urgent tiers are worth it—and when they are not

**Worth considering when:**

- Your departure is within a week and standard handling may not finish in time
- You discovered visa requirements late but still have a few days of margin
- Document quality is good and you need support tracking a tight timeline

**Poor value when:**

- You are applying **months** ahead—Normal tier is sufficient
- You have not gathered passport scans and photos yet—fix documents first
- You assume paying $220 bypasses immigration review—it does not

If you fly within **72 hours**, read [24-hour Vietnam eVisa guidance](/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa) and contact support immediately rather than assuming any tier is automatic.

## Single entry vs multiple entry: the $25 decision that saves hundreds

The government fee gap between single and multiple entry is **$25** ($55 vs $80). Choosing wrong entry type is one of the most expensive mistakes in Vietnam visa planning—because a second eVisa mid-trip costs the full government fee plus service fee again.

| Option | Government fee | Best for | Risk if you choose wrong |
| :--- | ---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Single entry** | **$55** | Fly in, travel inside Vietnam, fly out—no border exit | Cannot legally re-enter after a side trip to Cambodia, Thailand, or Laos |
| **Multiple entry** | **$80** | Regional loops, weekend exits, cruise re-embarkation | You pay $25 more upfront—but avoid a ~$115+ second application |

**Rule of thumb:** If your itinerary crosses a Vietnamese border even once and returns, plan **multiple entry** unless you hold another valid visa for the return. Full decision guide: [Vietnam multiple-entry eVisa guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-multiple-entry-guide-2026).

The visa invoice is only part of your pre-trip budget. Plan for these separately:

| Item | Typical impact | Planning note |

## Is an embassy visa cheaper than an eVisa?

Sometimes embassy **sticker** visas compete on government price alone—but total cost includes consulate travel, appointments, postage, time off work, and optional agent fees. For straightforward tourism and short business visits, online eVisas usually win on **speed and convenience**.

Embassy routes still matter for long-term work, study, diplomatic travel, and visa types not offered online. Full comparison: [Vietnam eVisa vs embassy visa](/blog/vietnam-evisa-vs-embassy-visa-2026). Embassy directory: [/embassy](/embassy).

| Scenario | Usually cheaper on paper | Usually faster in practice |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tourist, major airport entry, 2+ weeks lead time** | Official govt eVisa portal | Assisted or official eVisa |
| **Tourist, tomorrow's flight** | Neither—urgent tiers add cost | Assisted eVisa with rush tier (no guarantee) |
| **Work permit / 6-month stay** | Depends on consulate | Embassy processing |
| **Multiple entry, complex passport history** | Varies | Embassy consultation may save reapplication costs |

## Frequently asked questions

### How much does a Vietnam eVisa cost in 2026?

On our assisted portal: government fee (**$55** single entry or **$80** multiple entry for standard 90-day products) + **$59.99** service fee per passenger + any optional Urgent (**+$110**) or Super Urgent (**+$220**) processing. Official government-only filing costs less. Confirm your exact total at [/fees](/fees) and checkout before paying.

### What is the difference between government fee and service fee?

The **government fee** is mandatory and goes to Vietnamese immigration authorities for the visa authorization. The **service fee** pays for private assistance—form guidance, document validation, and support. It does not replace the government charge or guarantee approval.

### Is multiple entry more expensive than single entry?

Yes—**$80** government fee vs **$55** for single entry on the same 90-day products. The $25 difference is usually cheaper than applying for a second eVisa after an unplanned border exit.

### Are Vietnam eVisa fees refundable if I am rejected?

Government fees are often non-refundable once submitted. Service fees may be partially refundable under [/refund-policy](/refund-policy). Contact support with your reference number before bank chargebacks.

### Is the official government eVisa cheaper than assisted filing?

Yes. The official portal charges government fees only. Assisted filing adds the disclosed service fee for validation and support. Both submit to the same immigration review.

### Do children pay the same fees as adults?

On our portal, each passenger—including infants—pays the full government fee and service fee. Budget per person, not per household.

### Do urgent fees guarantee faster government approval?

No. Rush tiers speed preparation and submission on the assisted side. Final approval timing is decided by immigration authorities and can vary with holidays and document quality.

### What hidden costs should I budget beyond the visa?

Passport renewal, professional photos, printing, card FX fees, travel insurance, and potential extension fees inside Vietnam—all separate from the eVisa checkout total.
