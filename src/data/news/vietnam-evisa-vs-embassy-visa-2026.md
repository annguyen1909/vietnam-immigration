You can reach Vietnam through two main visa channels: a **Vietnam eVisa** filed online before travel, or a **visa from an embassy or consulate** (sticker in passport or loose-leaf insert). Neither wins every scenario. Picking the wrong channel costs money, weeks of delay, or—worst case—a refused boarding pass days before departure.

This 2026 comparison breaks down **cost, speed, stay length, multiple entry, entry ports, and purpose limits** so you choose the right path **before** non-refundable flights and hotels. It also clarifies what private assistance adds on top of official government fees—and when consular processing still matters.

## Embassy / consulate visa: when it still matters

Embassy visas are **physical stickers or inserts** issued by Vietnamese consulates after document review—often requiring **in-person visits or mailed passports**.

### Embassy strengths

- Visa types **not available** on the eVisa portal—long-term work, study, diplomatic, some special categories
- **Consular guidance** for unusual nationalities, prior refusals, or complex immigration history
- **Sticker in passport**—some employers and HR departments prefer traditional format
- Potentially **longer validity** or different entry conditions for frequent business commuters
- May suit travelers when **online multiple entry** is unavailable for their passport

### Embassy limitations

- **Appointments**, queues, or postal round trips
- Hidden costs: travel to consulate, courier, photos, time off work, **agent markups**
- Processing varies **widely by post**—London, Sydney, and Los Angeles timelines differ
- Passport **out of your hands** during processing—problematic if you travel internationally for work
- Still subject to **entry port and purpose rules** on the issued visa—not a universal "enter anywhere" pass

Directory: [/embassy](/embassy).

### Who should default to embassy

- **Employment contracts** requiring work permits or long-stay categories
- **University enrollment** and formal study programs
- **Official government or diplomatic travel**
- eVisa **unavailable** for your passport or purpose on [eligibility checker](/check-requirement)
- Prior **immigration violations** needing consular review
- Corporate HR mandates **sticker visa** format

## Entry ports: myth vs reality

A common misconception: "Embassy visas let me enter anywhere; eVisas are restricted." **Reality:** Both channels issue visas with **conditions**. eVisas clearly list ports on the PDF. Embassy stickers also specify entry limits for many categories.

**What actually matters:**

- Match **first international arrival** in Vietnam to the **authorized port** on your document
- Domestic flights after immigration are fine
- Land borders require the **named gate** on your visa

## Decision checklist

### Choose eVisa if you:

- Travel for **tourism** or **short business** (meetings, conferences, site visits)
- Enter via a major **airport or approved** land/seaport matching your ticket
- Need approval within **days**, not weeks
- Can match **passport data** and verify **entry port** on PDF exactly
- Want passport to **stay in your possession** during processing
- Hold nationality with **online multiple entry** available for regional loops

### Choose embassy if you:

- Need **long-term work, study, or residency** visas
- Were told eVisa is **unavailable** for your passport or purpose
- Require a visa type **not listed** on the online portal
- Have **complex prior immigration history** needing consular review
- Need **employer-mandated sticker format** or validity beyond online products
- Cannot meet eVisa **purpose or stay** rules for your actual activities

### When either channel works

Some nationalities and trips qualify for **both**. Compare:

1. **Total cost** including agents and courier
2. **Calendar time** to approval vs your departure date
3. **Entry port flexibility** on the issued document—not channel mythology
4. **Change risk** if itinerary shifts (eVisa port corrections are hard)

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a Vietnam eVisa better than an embassy visa?

For most short visits, yes—speed and convenience. Embassy wins for work, study, and visa types unavailable online.

### Can I get multiple entry online?

Many nationalities can—verify on [eligibility checker](/check-requirement) and [/fees](/fees). Embassy stickers remain the backup.

### Which channel fits your trip?

Compare **total** cost: government fee + service or agent fees + courier + travel time. For most eligible tourists, assisted eVisa at [/apply](/apply) is the faster, more convenient path.

### Can I switch from eVisa to embassy after rejection?

Yes—fix the rejection reason first. Avoid duplicate conflicting applications without advice.

### Does embassy visa allow more entry ports?

Not automatically. Both channels print conditions. Match your arrival to the authorized port on **your** visa.

### Can I work in Vietnam on an eVisa?

No. Tourist and standard short business eVisas do not authorize local employment.

### How long does each channel take?

Assisted eVisa Normal: commonly within five business days. Embassy: often one to three-plus weeks with mail.

### Should I use an embassy visa agent?

Optional. Agents add fees. For straightforward tourism, compare their total against assisted or official eVisa filing.
