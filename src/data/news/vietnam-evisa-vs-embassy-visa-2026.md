You can often choose between a **Vietnam eVisa** (online) and a **visa from an embassy or consulate** (sticker or loose-leaf). Neither replaces the other for every trip. This 2026 comparison helps you pick the right channel **before** non-refundable flights and hotels.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** We specialize in online eVisa preparation; embassy visas are handled by consulates—we can point you to requirements but do not replace consular officers.

## Quick comparison

| Factor | **eVisa (online)** | **Embassy / consulate** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Where you apply** | Home, any time | Consulate appointment or mail-in |
| **Typical speed** | Hours to few working days | Several days to 2+ weeks |
| **Entry ports** | Must match approved list | Depends on visa type |
| **Best for** | Tourism, short business | Work, study, some complex cases |
| **Passport handling** | Usually stays with you | May need to mail passport |

## Vietnam eVisa — best for most tourists

**Pros:**

- Apply from home; no consulate queue for standard trips.
- Digital approval by email—often **hours to a few working days**.
- Published fees online (government + optional service support).
- Strong fit for **tourism and short business** visits.

**Cons:**

- Entry limited to **authorized ports** on your approval.
- Stay length and single/multiple entry depend on options for your nationality.
- **Extensions inside Vietnam** are limited—not automatic.

**Ideal for:** holidaymakers, conference attendees, short digital-nomad stays, regional tourists entering via major airports.

## Embassy / consulate visa — when it still matters

**Pros:**

- Visa types **not available** on the eVisa portal (long-term work, study, some diplomatic cases).
- Familiar **sticker in passport** for employers with strict HR rules.
- Consular guidance for unusual nationalities or prior immigration history.

**Cons:**

- Appointments, mailing passports, or in-person visits.
- Extra costs: postage, photos, time off work, agent fees.
- Processing varies **widely by post**.

**Ideal for:** employment contracts, university enrollment, official travel, or when eVisa is unavailable for your passport.

## Decision checklist

**Choose eVisa if you:**

- Travel for tourism or short business.
- Enter via a major **airport or approved** land/seaport.
- Need approval within **days**, not weeks.
- Can match passport data and entry port exactly.

**Choose embassy if you:**

- Need **long-term work, study, or residency** visas.
- Were told eVisa is **unavailable** for your passport or purpose.
- Require a visa type **not listed** on the online portal.
- Have complex prior immigration history needing consular review.
