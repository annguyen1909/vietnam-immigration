Most foreign travelers need either a visa, eVisa, visa exemption, residence document, or another valid entry authorization before boarding a flight or crossing into Vietnam. The **Vietnam tourist visa valid for 90 days** is the most practical option for many travelers who are not covered by a short visa exemption or who need a longer stay.

Under Vietnam's current e-visa framework, citizens of [all countries and territories](https://evisa.gov.vn/) may apply for tourism or short business visits, with stays of up to **90 days** on single- or multiple-entry products ([Vietnam Immigration Department](https://immigration.gov.vn/)). ## Vietnam eVisa Passport Requirements in 2026

Your passport is the foundation of the application. Even a strong itinerary will not fix a passport mismatch.

| Requirement | What to prepare |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Passport validity** | At least 6 months beyond your intended arrival date is the safest standard |
| **Blank page** | At least one blank page for entry and exit stamps |

| **Name format** | Match your passport exactly, including middle names and order |

| **Travel document type** | Ordinary passports are the standard case; special documents may need extra review |

## Vietnam eVisa Photo Requirements 2026

Photo problems are one of the easiest ways to slow down an otherwise eligible application. Your portrait should look like a passport-style photo, not a selfie from your camera roll.

Use a recent color photo with a plain light background, neutral expression, open eyes, no sunglasses, and no heavy shadows. Keep the face centered and avoid cropping the top of the head, chin, ears, or shoulders too tightly. For children and infants, use a clean plain background and retake the image if the face is blurred.

For a full photo checklist, use our [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements guide for 2026](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026). It is worth checking before you upload because a five-minute reshoot can prevent days of avoidable delay.

## Vietnam eVisa Types: Single Entry vs Multi-Entry Visa Vietnam

Vietnam eVisas are available as single-entry or multiple-entry authorizations. The right choice depends on your route, not just the price.

| eVisa type | Best for | Watch out for |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Single-entry Vietnam eVisa** | One trip into Vietnam, then departure home or onward | Once you leave Vietnam, the visa is usually consumed |
| **Multi-entry visa Vietnam** | Trips that include Cambodia, Thailand, Laos, Singapore, or another country before returning to Vietnam | Your re-entry must still fall within the printed validity dates |

Choose multiple entry if your itinerary includes any international side trip and a return to Vietnam. A common mistake is booking Hanoi to Da Nang to Siem Reap to Ho Chi Minh City on a single-entry eVisa. That route leaves Vietnam and comes back, so it normally needs multiple entry or a second visa.

Our [Vietnam multiple-entry eVisa guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-multiple-entry-guide-2026) explains when the higher-cost option is worth it and how to avoid being blocked on your second arrival.

## Vietnam Visa Port of Entry Rules in 2026

Your **Vietnam visa port of entry** matters on the **approved eVisa PDF**, not as a field on our apply form. [Vietnamese immigration rules](https://immigration.gov.vn/) require you to arrive at an authorized airport, seaport, or land border that matches what is printed on your approval. Major airports such as [Noi Bai in Hanoi](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/hanoi), [Tan Son Nhat in Ho Chi Minh City](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/ho-chi-minh-city), and [Da Nang](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/da-nang) are common international gateways, but land and sea arrivals require extra care.

If your ticket lands in Ho Chi Minh City but the approval lists Hanoi, or if your cruise docks at a port not covered by your visa, you may face check-in problems or entry refusal. Airlines and border officers rely on the approved visa data, not on verbal explanations.

## Common Vietnam eVisa Mistakes That Cause Delays or Refusals

The most common problems are boring, tiny, and expensive in time. That is exactly why they matter.

- Passport number typed with one wrong digit
- Middle name omitted or entered in the wrong order
- Date of birth entered in the wrong format
- Passport scan cropped, blurry, dark, or missing MRZ lines
- Portrait photo rejected for shadows, glasses, filters, or background issues
- Single-entry eVisa selected for a multi-country itinerary
- Entry port on approval does not match ticket or border crossing
- Duplicate applications submitted before the first result is clear
- Travel purpose inconsistent with the selected visa type

## Vietnam eVisa Requirements for Families, Children, and Groups

Children and infants generally need their own travel authorization. Do not assume a child is covered by a parent's eVisa simply because the family travels together.

For families, prepare each passenger's passport details, portrait photo, and travel information before starting. If one child's photo fails or one passport number is wrong, the group can still be delayed. Families should apply earlier than solo travelers because there are more fields to check.

Read the [Vietnam eVisa guide for minors and families](/blog/vietnam-evisa-for-minors-families-2026) and the [family group Vietnam eVisa FAQ](/faq/family-group-vietnam-evisa) before submitting several passengers at once.

## Vietnam eVisa Requirements for Business, Work, and Long Stays

An eVisa can be suitable for short business meetings, conferences, market visits, or commercial discussions, depending on the purpose selected and your supporting itinerary. It is not a work permit and should not be used for paid employment inside Vietnam.

If you plan to work locally, study, stay long-term, join a Vietnamese employer, or enter under sponsorship, ask the relevant embassy, sponsor, or immigration authority before relying on a tourist eVisa. Choosing the wrong category can create problems at entry or later renewal.
