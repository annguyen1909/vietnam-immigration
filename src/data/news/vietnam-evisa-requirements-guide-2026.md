Most foreign visitors need **entry authorization** before flying to Vietnam. The **Vietnam eVisa** is the online route for many tourism and short business trips—but eligibility, stay length, and ports depend on your **nationality** and passport. Use this 2026 checklist before you book flights.

**We are a private visa assistance service—not the Government of Vietnam.** Policies are set by Vietnamese authorities; we help you prepare accurate applications and understand fees—not issue visas ourselves.

## Who needs a Vietnam eVisa?

Travelers who are **not visa-exempt** must hold a valid visa or eVisa before boarding. Some nationalities enter without a visa for short stays; others must apply online or at an embassy. Forum posts from past years are unreliable—confirm on [/check-requirement](/check-requirement) for your passport country.

- **Children and infants** normally need their own authorization.
- **Families** can often submit up to **15 passengers** in one application—[/faq/family-group-vietnam-evisa](/faq/family-group-vietnam-evisa).
- **Dual citizens** should apply with the passport they will use at the border.

## Passport and photo requirements

| Requirement | Standard practice |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Validity** | At least **6 months** beyond intended arrival |
| **Blank pages** | At least **one** for stamps |
| **Bio-page scan** | Colour, full page, corners visible, MRZ readable |
| **Portrait** | Plain background, neutral expression, recent likeness |
| **Email** | Inbox you monitor daily |
| **Payment** | Card for government fee (+ service fee if assisted) |

Blurry scans top the delay list. Re-shoot in daylight; do not upscale cropped screenshots. Photo rules: [/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026).

## Vietnam eVisa types and stay length

Vietnam eVisas in this flow are issued as **90-day** products, with **single** or **multiple** entry depending on the type you choose.

- **Single entry:** enter once within the validity window; a weekend in Thailand and return may require multiple entry or a new visa.
- **Multiple entry:** lawful re-entries within the approved period.
- **Business purpose:** use the correct purpose code; mismatched plans can trigger questions at the border.

## How long does processing take?

Standard processing is often quoted from **within 5 business days**, depending on volume and application quality. Holidays (**Tet**, summer peaks) add delay. Last-minute travel: [/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa](/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa). Approval is **never guaranteed** by your departure date.

## Common mistakes that delay or deny approval

- **Typos** on passport number or date of birth.
- **Wrong entry port** vs actual flight or land crossing.
- **Expired passport** or under six months validity.
- **Non-compliant photo** (filters, shadows, cropped ears).
- **Tourism visa** with obvious employment plans.
- **Duplicate applications** with conflicting data.

Fixing errors after submission costs time. Cross-check every field against the MRZ before payment.

## After approval: entry and extensions

Carry a **printed eVisa** plus the physical passport used in the application. Officers may ask about accommodation or return tickets.

**Extensions are not automatic.** Some travelers may apply at immigration offices in Vietnam before expiry, subject to approval and fees—conditions vary by visa type. Read extension notes in [/faq/vietnam-evisa-requirements](/faq/vietnam-evisa-requirements).

At least six months beyond arrival is the safe standard, with a blank page for stamps.

**Can I work on a tourist eVisa?**  
No. Paid work requires proper permits and visa types—often embassy routes.
