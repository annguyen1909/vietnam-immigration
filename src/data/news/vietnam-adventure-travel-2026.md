Adventure in Vietnam mixes **limestone scale** with **cultural density**: rappel inside colossal caverns one day, paddle misty rivers the next, and sweep **Ha Giang** switchbacks the day after. Operators quote tours in **VND**—confirm **helmets**, **insurance**, and **guide credentials** before you wire deposits.

## Phong Nha–Ke Bang caving

**Phong Nha–Ke Bang** hosts world-class expeditions plus shorter trips to **Paradise Cave**, **Dark Cave**, and **Tu Lan** swim-through days.

| Trip | Fitness | Cost band (VND) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Boardwalk show caves | Easy | 150,000–300,000 tickets |
| Tu Lan 1-day swim | Moderate | Tour packages, millions |
| Son Doong expedition | Elite | Tens of millions; porter teams |

**September–November**: verify flood advisories before river caves.

## Kayaking: Ha Long, Lan Ha, and Phong Nha rivers

- **Ha Long / Cat Ba**: **45–90 minute** kayak slots common on cruises—dry bags and UV gloves help
- **Phong Nha**: cornfield-backed paddles suit beginners when water levels cooperate
- **Always wear PFDs**—offshore winds change quickly

## Motorbike Ha Giang: loops without recklessness

The **Ha Giang** plateau rewards riders with **Ma Pi Leng** vistas and Hmong market colour.

### Safety essentials

- Valid **international motorcycle licence** matching displacement
- **Rain shells**—mountain fog drops visibility without warning
- Guided **easy-rider** trips: **~1,200,000–2,500,000 VND/day** (homestays often included)

Automatic scooters still demand traffic experience—consider a driver if Hanoi chaos is new to you.

## Safety, insurance, and recovery days

- List **caving/motorsports** explicitly on insurance
- Buffer a **rest day in Hanoi or HCMC** after humid multi-day caves—ear infections happen
- Photograph rental gear scratches at handover

## Sustainable adventure etiquette

Marked trails only; carry out wrappers; refuse coral or wildlife souvenirs. Tip porters in **VND** where cooperatives allow—some publish **no-tip** policies on lobby boards.
