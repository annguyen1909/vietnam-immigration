## Understanding Vietnamese dong (VND)

The dong uses **polymer and paper notes** from small denominations up to **500,000 VND**—roughly **20 USD** at typical 2026 rates, though rates move daily.

### Common notes you will handle

| Note (VND) | Rough USD (verify live) | Typical use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10,000 | ~$0.40 | Street snacks, small tips |
| 20,000 | ~$0.80 | Coffee, banh mi |
| 50,000 | ~$2 | Short Grab rides, market items |
| 100,000 | ~$4 | Sit-down lunch, museum entry |
| 200,000 | ~$8 | Dinner, scooter rental deposit |
| 500,000 | ~$20 | Hotel night (budget), large purchases |

**Count zeros carefully** when receiving change in busy markets—scammers exploit hurry at night markets and taxi curbs. Say amounts aloud when handing over 500,000 notes.

### Mental math for travellers

For quick estimates: **divide VND by 24,000–25,000** for USD, or **multiply USD by 25,000** for dong planning. Example: **200,000 VND lunch ≈ $8 USD**.

Use a notes app to log daily spends—humidity and jet lag make home-currency guessing drift fast on long trips.

### What you cannot do

- VND is **not freely convertible outside Vietnam**—spend or exchange back before departure (airport rates are poor for sell-back).
- **Coins** exist but rarely matter in tourist transactions—notes dominate.
- **Counterfeit risk** is low at licensed banks; avoid shady street changers promising unrealistic rates.

## Where to exchange money safely

**Licensed banks** (Vietcombank, BIDV, Agribank, Techcombank) and official exchange counters in **Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Da Nang** provide receipts and transparent rate boards.

### Best places to exchange

| Location | Rate quality | Convenience | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Licensed bank branch** | Best | Moderate (queues) | Passport often required; receipt provided |
| **Official exchange counter (tourist district)** | Good | High | Compare 2–3 boards within a block |
| **Hotel front desk** | Poor–fair | High | Convenience premium 1–3% |
| **Airport booth** | Fair for small amounts | Highest on arrival | OK for starter stack only |
| **Informal street desk** | Risky | Variable | Avoid unless you understand risks |

### Airport versus downtown

**Airport booths** suit a **500,000–2,000,000 VND** starter stack for taxis, SIM cards, and first meals. Larger sums earn better downtown rates once you reach your hotel district.

**Practical arrival flow:**

1. Land with small USD/EUR stack (optional)—$100–200 equivalent.
2. ATM withdraw **2,000,000–3,000,000 VND** at airport or first city stop if rates beat airport exchange.
3. Exchange remaining USD at a **licensed downtown bank** when you need more.

### USD, EUR, and other currencies

USD is the most widely accepted foreign cash for exchange. EUR and GBP work at major counters. Australian dollars, Japanese yen, and Korean won appear at tourist-district desks—rates vary. **Crisp notes** exchange easier than torn bills.

## ATMs and daily limits

ATMs cluster in urban centres, tourist wards, shopping malls, and airport arrivals. Major bank ATMs (Vietcombank, BIDV, Agribank, Techcombank, VPBank) generally accept **Visa and Mastercard debit cards**.

### What to expect at ATMs

- **Local ATM fees:** Often **20,000–50,000 VND** per withdrawal at some machines—fee shown on screen before confirmation.
- **Home bank fees:** Foreign transaction and out-of-network charges on top.
- **Per-withdrawal caps:** Commonly **2,000,000–3,000,000 VND**—plan multiple withdrawals or a bank visit for larger needs.
- **Daily card limits:** Set by your home bank—notify them before travel to reduce fraud blocks.

### ATM strategy for travellers

1. **Notify your bank** of Vietnam travel dates.
2. **Withdraw maximum allowed** per transaction to minimise fee stacking.
3. **Use ATMs inside bank branches** during business hours when possible—easier to resolve swallowed cards.
4. **Store card hotline numbers** outside your wallet.
5. **Carry two cards** from different networks (e.g., Visa + Mastercard).

### Where ATMs disappear

Withdraw dong **before** remote routes:

- **Ha Giang Loop** and far northern passes
- **Ba Be** and remote national parks
- **Con Dao** and smaller islands (ATMs exist but queues and outages happen on holidays)
- **Mountain homestays** between major towns

## Cards and mobile payments

### Credit and debit cards

**Visa and Mastercard** work at:

- International hotels and upscale restaurants
- Airlines and major booking platforms
- Larger cafés in Hanoi, HCMC, Da Nang tourist cores
- Some supermarket chains (Big C, WinMart)

**Cash still required for:**

- Street food and market stalls
- Rural homestays and village meals
- Ferries, pier fees, and island shuttles
- Marine park gates and many tour operators
- Grab cash trips (though Grab accepts cards in cities)
- Motorbike rental deposits (often cash)

### Dynamic currency conversion (DCC)

When a POS terminal offers charge in **USD or home currency** instead of VND—**choose VND**. DCC rarely favours travellers; your home bank's rate is usually better.

### Domestic mobile wallets

**MoMo, ZaloPay, and VNPay** dominate local life but require Vietnamese bank accounts tourists typically lack. Short-term visitors rely on **cash + one widely accepted card**. QR payments at street stalls are uncommon without local wallet setup.

## Tipping and service charges

Tipping is **appreciation, not obligation**—but tourism hubs increasingly expect modest gratuities.

| Situation | Norm in 2026 | Suggested range (VND) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Street food / cafés | Round up or leave small change | 5,000–20,000 |
| Taxis (Grab or meter) | Round to nearest 10,000 | 10,000–30,000 |
| Guided tours / boat crews | If pleased with service | 50,000–200,000 per day |
| Hotel housekeeping | Not required | 20,000–50,000 per night |
| Restaurants | Check bill first | 5–10% if no service charge |
| Spa / massage | Optional | 50,000–100,000 |

**Check restaurant bills** for included **5–10% service charge** before tipping twice. Applause often suffices at community performances—cash tips are not expected.

| Cat Ba / Got Pier ferry | 80,000–120,000 per leg | Rarely |

**Rule:** Withdraw **2,000,000–5,000,000 VND** on the mainland before island hops. Carry **50,000 and 100,000 notes** for pier fees—500,000 notes are hard to change on boats.

## Practical saving and security tips

- **Split cash** across pockets and hotel safe—don't carry everything to the beach.
- **Photograph ATM receipts** until home bank balances reconcile.
- **Refuse redundant plastic bags** at markets—mesh totes survive monsoon afternoons.
- **Buy sunscreen and reef-safe lotion in city pharmacies**—island and resort markups hurt.
- **Compare tour prices in VND** before USD-quoted resort desks—conversion spreads hide premiums.
- **Use hotel safes** for passport and excess cash; carry copies of eVisa approval PDF separately.

## Common money mistakes in Vietnam

1. **Relying on cards at island piers**—marine fees and snacks are cash-first.
2. **Exchanging entire budget at the airport**—downtown banks beat spreads on large sums.
3. **Accepting DCC in USD at POS**—choose VND settlement.
4. **Single ATM withdrawal per day**—fees stack; withdraw the daily max.
5. **Forgetting to notify home bank**—fraud blocks strand you without cash.
6. **Miscounting zeros** on 500,000 notes in dark markets—say amounts aloud.
7. **Tipping twice** when service charge already on bill.
8. **Booking non-refundable travel before eVisa approval**—sort [how to apply for a Vietnam eVisa online](/blog/how-to-apply-vietnam-evisa-online-2026) first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Both.** Small USD/EUR stack for arrival convenience; **ATMs in cities** for larger VND amounts at competitive effective rates. Licensed downtown exchange beats airport spreads on big conversions.

### Do ATMs in Vietnam accept foreign debit cards?

Yes at major bank ATMs in tourist cities. Expect **local fees (20,000–50,000 VND)**, home bank charges, and **2,000,000–3,000,000 VND caps** per withdrawal.

### Can I pay with credit cards everywhere in Vietnam?

No. Cards work at upscale hotels and larger restaurants; **street food, ferries, markets, and park fees** need cash. Carry VND and a backup card.

### Is tipping expected in Vietnam in 2026?

Not mandatory, but **50,000–200,000 VND** tips for guides and **20,000–50,000 VND** for housekeeping are appreciated in tourism. Check for service charges on restaurant bills.

### How much cash should I carry for island trips?

Plan **500,000–2,000,000 VND** per day depending on activities. Marine park fees, ferries, and village meals rarely take cards—withdraw on the mainland first.
