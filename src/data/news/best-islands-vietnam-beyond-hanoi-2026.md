## How Vietnam's islands differ by region

Vietnam is an S-shaped coast, not a single beach belt. Island quality, water clarity, and logistics change sharply by latitude:

- **Gulf of Tonkin (north):** Dramatic **limestone karsts**, cooler winter air, and ferry-dependent access. Water is not Caribbean-blue but the scenery is unmatched. Ideal for travellers based in **Hanoi** who want islands without flying south.
- **Central coast:** Smaller protected islets with **snorkelling** and fishing villages. Seasonal—autumn typhoon tracks can shut boats for days. Best combined with **Hoi An**, **Da Nang**, or **Hue**.
- **Southern marine frontier:** **Phu Quoc** (Gulf of Thailand) and **Con Dao** (South China Sea) deliver warmer water, longer beaches, and flight-dependent itineraries. Worth the hop if you have a full week for the south.

Choosing wrong is the most common mistake: booking **Phu Quoc** for a three-day Hanoi add-on burns two days in airports. Conversely, skipping south entirely when you have ten days leaves Vietnam's best sand and diving unexplored.

## Cat Ba Archipelago and Lan Ha Bay

**Cat Ba** is the heavyweight northern choice. The island town sits at the edge of **Cat Ba National Park**, with trails, viewpoints, and beaches that reward two to four nights. Most visitors pair land time with a **Ha Long Bay** or **Lan Ha Bay** cruise—Lan Ha is the quieter sibling on Cat Ba's south flank, with fewer mega-junks and stronger kayaking.

### How to reach Cat Ba from Hanoi

| Route | Duration | Typical cost (VND) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bus to Hai Phong + Got ferry | 3–4 hours door to pier | 150,000–250,000 bus; 80,000–120,000 ferry | Most common; book ferry times ahead on weekends |
| Private transfer + speedboat | 2.5–3 hours | 1,500,000–3,000,000 per car | Faster; worth it for tight schedules |
| Organised cruise (Hanoi pickup) | 2–3 days | 1,500,000–4,000,000+ per person | Includes bay meals and kayaking |

**Got Pier (Phà Gót)** and **Tuan Chau** (Ha Long side) are the main ferry gateways. Weekend traffic from Hanoi escapees fills morning sailings—arrive early or book online where operators allow it.

### What to do on Cat Ba

- **Cat Ba National Park:** Forest trails to **Ngu Lam Peak** and **Hospital Cave** (a wartime field hospital inside the mountain). Park entry often runs **60,000–80,000 VND**.
- **Lan Ha kayaking:** Paddle through floating villages and narrow karst channels. Half-day rentals or cruise-included sessions are standard.
- **Beaches:** **Cat Co 1, 2, 3** coves are walkable from town; weekends get loud. Quieter options exist on the island's east and south rims—ask your hotel about scooter access.
- **Overnight cruises:** One-night trips suit checklist travellers; **two-night routes** reach quieter moorings. Ask about passenger caps, engine policies, and whether kayaking is included or extra. Budget **1,500,000–4,000,000 VND** per person depending on cabin class.

### Cat Ba: who it suits and realistic pros/cons

**Best for:** Hanoi-based travellers, photographers, kayakers, and anyone who wants **limestone drama** without flying to the south.

**Pros:** No domestic flight required from Hanoi; strong national park hiking; cruises deliver iconic Vietnam imagery.

**Cons:** Water clarity is moderate—not top snorkelling. Winter **northeast monsoon** (roughly November–February) brings chop that delays ferries and cruises. Town development is mixed; pick eco-minded operators.

**Typical mistake:** Booking a one-night cruise and Cat Ba hiking in a single two-day weekend—you will rush both. Allow three nights minimum.

## Cu Lao Cham (Cham Islands) from Hoi An

**Cu Lao Cham** is a marine protected area fifteen kilometres off **Hoi An**. Unlike Cat Ba's scale, Cham is a **day-trip or short homestay** destination—clearer water for snorkelling, coral gardens, and regulated beaches with fishing-village meals.

### Getting to the Cham Islands

Speedboats leave from **Cua Dai** or central **Hoi An** piers. Crossing time is usually **30–45 minutes** when seas cooperate. Operators run morning departures with afternoon returns; overnight homestays on **Bai Huong** or **Bai Lang** villages let you see the island after day-trippers leave.

**Marine and pier fees** often total **50,000–200,000 VND**—carry small notes. Confirm last return boats; **afternoon wind and chop** frequently delay departures, especially outside the calmer **May–August** window.

### Cham Islands: planning essentials

| Factor | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Ideal trip length** | 1 day (snorkel tour) or 1–2 nights (homestay) |
| **Best season** | May–August for calmer central seas; check weekly forecasts always |
| **Worst season** | September–December—autumn storms pause boats for days |
| **Budget** | Day tours from **400,000–800,000 VND**; homestays **200,000–400,000 VND** per night plus meals |
| **Who it suits** | Central-coast itineraries, snorkellers, culture + beach combos |

**Snorkelling note:** Coral is recovering from past pressure—float fins above reefs, do not stand on coral heads, and follow guide instructions. Marine park rules are enforced locally; fines and tour cancellations apply for violations.

**Route logic:** Base in **Hoi An** or **Da Nang**, not Hanoi. Fly or train to **Da Nang International Airport (DAD)**—confirm it matches your [Vietnam eVisa entry port](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026) if DAD is your first landing in Vietnam.

## Phu Quoc: beaches, pepper farms, and night markets

**Phu Quoc** is Vietnam's largest island—a **southern beach week** destination with domestic flights from **Ho Chi Minh City (SGN)** and seasonal links from **Hanoi (HAN)**. Resort strips line the west coast; the interior hides **pepper plantations**, fish-sauce warehouses, and **Phu Quoc National Park** trails.

### How to reach Phu Quoc

- **Flights:** **Phu Quoc International Airport (PQC)** receives daily services from SGN (≈1 hour) and less frequent HAN routes (≈2 hours). Book early for Tet and summer holiday peaks.
- **Ferry (alternative):** Ha Tien or Rach Gia ferries reach the island—slower, scenic, and useful for overland travellers from Cambodia, but most international visitors fly.

### What to do on Phu Quoc

- **Long Beach (Bai Truong):** Broad sunset sands near Duong Dong town; resorts and cafés line the strip.
- **Sao Beach and southern coves:** Whiter sand, busier midday—arrive early.
- **Duong Dong Night Market:** Grilled seafood priced by weight in VND—confirm totals before cooking. A full meal often runs **150,000–350,000 VND** per person.
- **Snorkel and dive day boats:** Southern islets and **An Thoi** archipelago trips; pair with our [Vietnam diving destinations guide](/blog/vietnam-best-diving-destinations-2026) if scuba is on your list.
- **Inland:** Pepper farm tours (**50,000–100,000 VND**), **Dinh Cau** temple at the harbour, and prison museum history for context.

### Phu Quoc: who it suits and realistic pros/cons

**Best for:** Families, resort travellers, first-time beach weeks, and anyone wanting **easy logistics** with frequent flights.

**Pros:** Long beaches, strong food scene, year-round warmth, good airport access.

**Cons:** Rapid development—some beaches feel commercial. Rainy afternoons occur even in "dry" season. Not a solitude island; crowds peak on domestic holidays.

**Ideal length:** **4–7 nights.** Shorter than four nights and you spend disproportionate time on transport.

**Budget (mid-range, excluding flights):** **1,000,000–2,500,000 VND** per day for meals, scooter rental, and activities. Luxury resorts push higher; hostel and guesthouse travellers can spend less.

## Con Dao: national park, history, and frontier diving

**Con Son**—the main inhabited island of the **Con Dao archipelago**—feels like a different country from Phu Quoc. Limited flights preserve a slower tempo. **Con Dao National Park** protects forests, turtle nesting beaches, and offshore dive sites around **Hon Bay Canh** and neighbouring islets.

### How to reach Con Dao

**Con Dao Airport (VCS)** receives flights from **SGN** (≈1 hour)—typically one to three daily services depending on season. There is no practical ferry for most international travellers. From Hanoi, route **HAN → SGN → VCS** and allow **buffer nights** in HCMC if connections are tight.

### What to do on Con Dao

- **Beaches:** **Dam Trau**, **An Hai**, and **Lo Voi** offer quiet sand; currents vary—ask locally before swimming alone.
- **Diving and snorkelling:** Licensed operators run park-compliant trips; visibility and currents change daily. Strong divers reward Con Dao; beginners should confirm site difficulty. See our [diving guide](/blog/vietnam-best-diving-destinations-2026) for season notes.
- **History:** **Con Dao Prison** complex documents colonial and wartime incarceration—sobering but essential context.
- **Turtle nesting:** Seasonal nesting (often **May–September**) demands **red-light beach protocols** after dark—no flash photography, follow ranger briefings.

### Con Dao: who it suits and realistic pros/cons

**Best for:** Divers, wildlife watchers, slow travellers, and visitors who prioritise **quiet** over nightlife.

**Pros:** Marine park protection, turtle nesting, uncrowded beaches, strong diving potential.

**Cons:** Expensive relative to Phu Quoc; few flights; activities cancel when currents spike. Not ideal for a two-day add-on.

**Ideal length:** **3–5 nights** with one spare day for weather cancellations.

**Typical mistake:** Booking Con Dao and Phu Quoc back-to-back with one night in between—flight schedules make this fragile. Choose one southern island per trip unless you have eight or more southern days.

## Northern alternatives worth knowing: Ly Son and Co To

Travellers who have already done Cat Ba sometimes want **smaller, less cruise-ship northern islands**:

### Ly Son (Quang Ngai)

A volcanic island famous for **garlic farms**, cliff temples, and **To Vo Gate** rock arch. Reach via train or bus to **Quang Ngai**, then a **45–60 minute ferry**. Best **March–September**; typhoon season disrupts sailings. Two nights is enough. Budget travellers appreciate homestay rates around **200,000–400,000 VND** per night. Fewer tours in English—download offline maps.

### Co To (Quang Ninh)

Remote sands northeast of **Ha Long** territory. Ferries run from **Van Don** or **Cai Rong** piers on **seasonal schedules**—verify current timetables before committing. Foreign tourist infrastructure is thinner than Cat Ba; cash is essential. Rewards travellers who accept basic lodging for empty beaches.

These are **secondary picks**—choose them when you want fewer junks and accept less English signage, not when you have only one island slot on a first visit.

## Choosing the right island: decision guide

Ask four questions before booking:

1. **Where are you based?** Hanoi → Cat Ba. Hoi An/Da Nang → Cham. HCMC or flying south → Phu Quoc or Con Dao.
2. **How many days?** Under four → one northern or central island only. Seven to ten → one southern island week. Twelve plus → north + south with a flight bridge.
3. **What matters more—scenery or sand?** Karst and kayaking → Cat Ba. White sand and resorts → Phu Quoc. Marine life and solitude → Con Dao.
4. **What season are you travelling?** Never ignore **central autumn storms** or **winter Gulf of Tonkin chop**. Check forecasts weekly, not just monthly averages.

| --- | --- | --- |
| No domestic flight from Hanoi | Cat Ba | Co To (adventurous) |

| Family resort week | Phu Quoc | Da Nang (mainland, not island) |
| Diving + turtles | Con Dao | Phu Quoc (easier logistics) |
| Photography / karsts | Lan Ha / Cat Ba | Ly Son cliffs |

## Sample island-hopping itineraries

### North long weekend (from Hanoi)

**Days 1–2:** Bus to Hai Phong, ferry to Cat Ba, national park hike or Hospital Cave.

**Day 3:** Lan Ha kayak day or overnight cruise embarkation.

**Day 4:** Return ferry, bus to Hanoi.

**Transport:** Bus + ferry only. **Budget:** **2,500,000–5,000,000 VND** per person excluding Hanoi hotels.

### Central culture + sea (10 days)

**Days 1–3:** Da Nang base, Marble Mountains, food tour.

**Days 4–6:** Hoi An old town, bicycle villages, **Cham Islands** day or overnight.

**Days 7–8:** Hue imperial sites via Hai Van Pass.

**Days 9–10:** Fly to next region or depart from DAD.

### Southern beach reset (7–10 days)

**Days 1–5:** Fly to **Phu Quoc**—west-coast beaches, night market, one snorkel day.

**Optional Days 6–8:** Fly to **Con Dao** only if you accept limited schedules and higher cost—or extend Phu Quoc instead.

**Rule:** Fly south **once**. Avoid Hanoi → Phu Quoc → Hanoi → Cat Ba in one short trip.

1. **Treating Phu Quoc as a Hanoi side trip** in under five days—you lose half the holiday to airports.
2. **Ignoring return boat times** on Cham Islands—afternoon chop strands overnight unintentionally (carry cash if you must extend).
3. **Booking non-refundable cruises in central autumn** without weather flexibility.
4. **Assuming cards work everywhere**—village ferries and park gates are cash-first.

6. **Choosing Con Dao for a party beach week**—nightlife is minimal; Phu Quoc fits that mood better.
7. **Standing on coral** while snorkelling—damages reefs and risks fines in protected areas.

## Practical logistics: ferries, flights, connectivity

- **SIM data:** Buy a mainland **tourist SIM** before island hops—limestone shadows and open water weaken signal on boats.
- **Motion sickness:** Gulf crossings and speedboats reward medication taken **30 minutes before** departure.
- **Dry bags:** Essential for kayak days and monsoon-season deck travel.
- **Insurance:** Ferry and snorkel activities may not suit bare-minimum medical policies—confirm coverage for water sports.
- **Domestic flights:** Vietnam's airlines price dynamically; island routes sell out on holiday weekends. Book Phu Quoc and Con Dao legs before boutique hotels, not after.

## Frequently asked questions

### What are the best islands in Vietnam for first-time visitors?

**Phu Quoc** is the easiest first island—flights, resorts, and English signage are strongest. **Cat Ba** is best if you start in **Hanoi** and want iconic karst scenery without flying south. **Con Dao** suits second-time visitors who prioritise diving and quiet over convenience.

### Can I visit islands on a Vietnam eVisa?

Yes. The eVisa covers entry to Vietnam; domestic ferries and flights between islands do not require separate permits for standard tourism. Ensure your **passport validity** meets official requirements and your entry port matches your international arrival.

### How many days should I spend on Vietnamese islands?

**Cat Ba:** 2–4 nights. **Cham Islands:** 1 day or 1–2 nights. **Phu Quoc:** 4–7 nights. **Con Dao:** 3–5 nights. Shorter stays work only when the island is one stop in a longer coast itinerary—not when it is your main beach goal.

### Is island hopping in Vietnam expensive?

It can be mid-range affordable by Southeast Asian standards. Ferries and speedboats add cost; southern flights are the largest expense from Hanoi. Budget **800,000–2,500,000 VND** per day on-island for mid-range travellers, excluding long-haul domestic flights.

### Which Vietnam island has the clearest water?

**Con Dao** and **Cham Islands** (in season) often beat Gulf of Tonkin clarity. **Phu Quoc** varies with plankton and weather. **Cat Ba** prioritises scenery over translucent water—set expectations accordingly.

### Are there direct flights from Hanoi to Phu Quoc?

Seasonal direct **HAN–PQC** services exist but schedules change—check current timetables when booking. Routing via **SGN** remains reliable year-round.

### What should I pack for Vietnam island trips?

Reef-safe sunscreen, quick-dry clothing, brimmed hat, sandals with grip for wet piers, motion-sickness remedy, insect repellent, light rain shell, and **VND cash in small notes**. Waterproof phone pouches save devices during kayak and squall days.

### Is it safe to swim at Vietnam beaches?

Swim in **flagged zones** when lifeguards operate. Karst channels and island headlands hide currents. Jellyfish pulses occur seasonally—ask locals before dawn swims. Marine park rules may restrict certain beaches during turtle nesting.

Vietnam's best islands are not interchangeable. **Cat Ba and Lan Ha** reward Hanoi-based travellers with karst kayaking and national park trails. **Cu Lao Cham** delivers accessible snorkelling from **Hoi An** when seas are calm. **Phu Quoc** anchors easy southern beach weeks; **Con Dao** trades convenience for marine parks, diving, and silence.
