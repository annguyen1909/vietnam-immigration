Vietnam’s islands scatter across the **Gulf of Tonkin**, the **central coast**, and the **southern marine frontier**—each cluster delivers a different rhythm: junk boats and limestone towers within reach of Hanoi, snorkel day trips from **Hoi An**, or week-long beach resets beside coral-fringed reserves in the south.

## Cat Ba Archipelago and Lan Ha Bay

Travellers short on time pair **Ha Long Bay** imagery with **Cat Ba town** hotels and beaches. Ferries and cruises link the island to **Hai Phong** and bay routes; kayaking quiet **Lan Ha** corners reveals rocky gardens away from larger vessel lanes.

### What to do and typical costs

- **Cat Ba National Park** viewpoints: entry often **60,000–80,000 VND**
- **Hospital Cave** and cannon ridge trails add historical texture between swims
- **Overnight cruises** from **1,500,000–4,000,000 VND** per person depending on cabin class—ask about passenger caps and engine policies

Expect mixed development—choose eco-minded operators when booking. Weekends fill fast from Hanoi escape traffic.

## Cu Lao Cham (Cham Islands) from Hoi An

Fast boats bring you to **protected Cu Lao Cham** waters where snorkelling follows central Vietnam’s clearer months (often **May–August**). Homestays suit overnights; day trips focus on snorkel stops, fishing villages, and regulated beaches.

**Marine fees** may apply—carry **50,000–200,000 VND** in small notes for pier charges. Confirm last return boats; afternoon chop can delay departures.

## Phu Quoc: beaches, pepper, and night markets

Phu Quoc blends international resorts with **pepper plantations** and fishing harbours. **Long Beach** suits sunset walks; **Duong Dong** town hums at night with seafood priced in VND—confirm weight before grilling.

Domestic flights from **Ho Chi Minh City** make tight itineraries workable. Balance resort time with southern coves when schedules allow; sunrise fish markets reveal island livelihoods before heat builds.

## Con Dao National Park character

**Con Son island** contrasts history museums and former penal colony sites with forested interiors and diving around **Hon Bay Canh**. Limited flights preserve slower tempo—book accommodation early, respect **marine park rules**, and use licensed dive centres.

Turtle nesting seasons demand **red-light beach protocols** after dark. Strong currents occasionally postpone offshore sites—pad spare days.

## Sample island-hopping sequences

| Route | Flow | Transport |
|-------|------|-----------|
| North long weekend | Hanoi → Hai Phong → Cat Ba → Hanoi | Bus + ferry |
| Central culture + sea | Da Nang → Hoi An → Cham day trip → Hue | Train/bus + boat |
| Southern reset | HCMC → Phu Quoc → optional Con Dao | Flights |

Avoid backtracking: fly south once if Phu Quoc or Con Dao anchors your beach block.

## Planning ferries, flights, and weather

Central coast seas vary seasonally; **autumn storms** can pause Cham boats. Southern islands skew drier mid-year but crowds peak. Northeast monsoon chop delays **Lan Ha** junk departures—buffer a night in **Hai Phong** when onward flights loom.

Buy mainland **SIM data** before remote hops; limestone shadows weaken signal on boats.
