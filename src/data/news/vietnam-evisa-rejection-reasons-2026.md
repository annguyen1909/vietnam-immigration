## How Vietnam eVisa review works (and why refusals feel vague)

After you pay, your application enters an immigration review queue managed by [Vietnamese immigration authorities](https://immigration.gov.vn/). Early stages often use **automated document checks**—file format, image clarity, MRZ readability, and basic field validation. Cases that pass automation may receive human review. Security and eligibility databases can flag applications regardless of how clean your uploads look.

Refusal emails frequently use **generic language** without specifying whether the photo, passport number, or eligibility screen failed. That is official process—not necessarily a sign your case is hopeless. Your job is to diagnose which rejection category fits, fix it, and reapply with corrected data.

## Document quality rejections

Automated checks scan uploads before or during human review. Typical failures:

### Cropped or incomplete passport scans

The **machine-readable zone (MRZ)**—two lines at the bottom of the bio page—must be fully visible. Cutting off a corner, covering edges with fingers, or photographing at an angle makes the MRZ unreadable.

**Fix:** Lay the passport flat on a dark surface. Photograph in daylight with all four corners visible. Zoom to 100% on your screen and confirm every MRZ character is sharp before upload.

### Glare, shadow, and colour problems

Flash hotspots across the photo page, shadows from your hand, or heavy filters make automated OCR fail.

**Fix:** Turn off flash. Use indirect natural light. Re-scan rather than editing brightness so aggressively that text blurs.

### Portrait photo failures

Selfies, sunglasses, patterned backgrounds, hats (non-religious), heavy beauty filters, and group photos fail passport-style requirements.

**Fix:** Plain light background, face forward, eyes open, recent likeness. Full rules: [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026).

### Wrong file type, size, or compression

Over-compressed JPGs turn MRZ lines into illegible blocks. Wrong file types may not upload correctly or may fail validation silently.

**Fix:** Export a high-quality JPG within portal size limits. **Do not upscale** a blurry image—officers compare pixels to your physical passport at entry.

## Data mismatch rejections

Every typed field must mirror the passport you will carry at immigration. Mismatches are among the most common rejection—and boarding denial—causes.

| Field | Common error | How to fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Passport number** | Dropped leading zeros; letter O vs zero | Read MRZ character by character |
| **Full name** | Missing middle name; wrong order | Copy exactly from bio page visual zone |
| **Date of birth** | US month/day habit vs DD/MM/YYYY | Use passport print format, not memory |
| **Nationality** | Country of residence instead of passport country | Select passport nationality |
| **Sex / gender** | M/F does not match passport code | Match bio page coding |

**Critical point:** An approval with a typo is not a reliable travel document. Airlines match eVisa records to the passport number in their departure control system. If the numbers disagree, you may be rejected even after an "approval" email.

Cross-check the MRZ before every payment. Our [common mistakes guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-common-mistakes-2026) works as a final pre-pay audit.

## Eligibility and policy rejections

You may be refused when your profile does not match eVisa policy for the purpose and passport you submitted:

- **Nationality not eligible** for the selected visa purpose (confirm on [Vietnam visa eligibility by country](/check-requirement))
- **Diplomatic or official passport** requiring embassy processing
- **Tourism selected** but travel pattern or history suggests unauthorized employment
- Prior **overstay, deportation, or immigration violation** on record
- **Visa-exempt** nationality used incorrectly when an eVisa was unnecessary—or exemption length exceeded

**Planning advice:** Broad eVisa eligibility applies to many nationalities in 2026, but exemptions and bilateral rules still matter. A friend with a different passport may not be a valid comparison.

If eVisa is not the right channel, compare [Vietnam eVisa vs embassy visa](/blog/vietnam-evisa-vs-embassy-visa-2026) before paying again.

## Security and fraud screening rejections

Sudden refusals with minimal explanation sometimes involve security review rather than a typo you can see:

### Duplicate applications

Submitting two paid applications for the same passport because the first "felt slow" creates **conflicting records**. Systems may flag duplicates as fraud risk, delaying or refusing both.

**Fix:** Track one reference number. Contact support with payment proof before opening a parallel application.

### Stolen passport reports

If your passport was reported lost or stolen internationally, databases may block approval until resolved with your passport office.

**Fix:** Contact your issuing authority before reapplying. Do not submit a third duplicate "just in case."

### Rapid payment attempts and unusual access patterns

Multiple card attempts from VPN or datacenter IPs in a short window can trigger automated holds.

**Fix:** Wait for support guidance. Use a stable connection and one payment method when possible.

## Wrong visa product selected

Selecting the wrong product can cause rejection—or approval followed by **entry denial** when your real itinerary does not match the PDF.

### Single entry vs multiple entry

| Product | Government fee (our portal) | Risk if wrong |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| **Single entry** | **$55** | Cannot re-enter after leaving Vietnam |
| **Multiple entry** | **$80** | Higher fee—but required for leave-and-return loops |

Side trips to Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, or cruise re-boarding usually need **multiple entry**. Read [Vietnam multiple-entry eVisa guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-multiple-entry-guide-2026) before reapplying.

### Tourism vs business purpose

Selecting tourism for meetings, factory visits, or commercial activity you should declare as business can fail purpose checks. Tourism does **not** authorize local employment.

### Entry port mismatch

Your approved PDF lists a specific **port of entry**. Arriving elsewhere is an entry violation—not something you can fix at the immigration desk with a different airport ticket.

Read [Vietnam eVisa entry ports](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026) and align your route before reapplying.

## Payment and technical issues

Less common than document errors, but real:

- **Card charged** but application stuck "pending"—no reference email received
- **Email typo** on the form—approval sent to wrong inbox while you wait on the right one
- **Browser timeout** during upload—partial file saved without obvious error
- **Double charge** without two distinct reference numbers—support must reconcile

**Fix:** Save your **application reference** and payment confirmation immediately. Contact support before submitting a parallel paid application.

## What to do after a Vietnam eVisa rejection

Follow this sequence before you pay again:

1. **Read every email** from authorities and your assistance provider for correction hints.
2. **Classify the rejection**—document, data, eligibility, product, security, or technical.
3. **Fix the root cause**—new photo, corrected MRZ fields, right visa type—not identical resubmission.
4. **Review** [/refund-policy](/refund-policy) for fee treatment before disputing with your bank.
5. **Wait 24–48 hours** if the portal shows a cooling-off period (policy varies).
6. **Reapply** on [/apply](/apply) with corrected data—or consider embassy processing for complex cases.

**Do not board** your flight hoping immigration will overlook a rejection. Airlines check authorization at check-in.

## Entry denied despite "approval"

An approval PDF does not guarantee entry if real-world facts disagree with the authorization:

| Issue | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Arrival at **non-listed port** | Entry refused |
| Passport **validity under six months** | Boarding or entry denied |
| **Wrong passport** presented (renewed after applying) | eVisa not valid for new booklet |
| **Unauthorized work** on tourist visa | Immigration questioning or refusal |
| **Single entry** after you already exited | Re-entry denied |

Carry a **printed copy** and digital backup. Arrive early if you know there is a minor discrepancy—but do not rely on exceptions.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why was my Vietnam eVisa rejected with no explanation?

Generic refusals usually point to document quality, data mismatches, eligibility, wrong visa product, or security screening. Diagnose the category from your uploads and form data before paying again.

### Can I reapply immediately after rejection?

Often yes after fixes. Avoid identical resubmissions and respect any cooling-off message in the portal.

### Will I get a refund if my Vietnam eVisa is rejected?

Government fees are often non-refundable. Service fees may qualify under [/refund-policy](/refund-policy). Contact support with your reference number first.

### Can I still fly without approval?

No. Secure a new eVisa or another valid entry document before check-in.

### Can a typo in my passport number cause rejection?

Yes—or worse, approval with wrong data that fails at airline check-in. Always verify against the MRZ.

### Does paying twice speed up approval after rejection?

No. Duplicate applications can worsen security screening. Fix one application or wait for formal refusal before reapplying.

### How long should I wait before reapplying?

If the portal shows no cooling-off period, reapply as soon as documents and data are corrected. If instructed to wait, respect the stated window.

### What if I am rejected close to my flight date?

Contact support immediately. Review [24-hour Vietnam eVisa guidance](/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa) and urgent processing tiers only if your arrival date allows. Do not book non-refundable plans until you hold a valid PDF.
