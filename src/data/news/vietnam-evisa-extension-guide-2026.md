Your **Vietnam eVisa** prints a fixed **validity window**, **entry type**, and **authorized stay length**—not an open-ended hotel checkout you can extend with one click. Many travelers search "extend Vietnam eVisa" after their plans change, assuming the same online portal handles extra days. In most cases it does not. Extensions, when permitted at all, are handled at **immigration offices inside Vietnam** before your current stay expires, subject to officer approval, supporting documents, and fees paid in local currency.

This 2026 guide explains what you can and cannot extend, where applications are filed, typical costs and timelines, overstay consequences, and the safer alternative: **choosing the right eVisa duration and entry type before you fly**.

## What your Vietnam eVisa actually authorizes

Before discussing extensions, understand three dates on your approval PDF—they are not interchangeable.

| Term | What it means | Extension relevance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Visa validity window** | Period during which you may **enter** Vietnam | Entering after this window is not allowed—even with days "left" on paper |
| **Duration of stay** | Maximum days you may **remain** after each entry stamp | This is what extensions attempt to lengthen—if policy allows |
| **Entry type** | **Single** (one arrival period) vs **multiple** (leave and return within validity) | Wrong entry type cannot be fixed by extension—you may need exit and re-entry |

Under Vietnam's current e-visa framework, standard online products authorize stays of **up to 90 days** ([Vietnam Electronic Visa portal](https://evisa.gov.vn/)). That is the maximum duration available at initial filing on most portals—not a guarantee that an in-country extension will add another 90 days later.

**Official policy signal:** Vietnamese immigration authorities set permitted stay lengths and extension rules. **Planning advice:** Treat your printed exit date as a hard deadline until an immigration officer formally approves an extension stamp or slip.

## Can you extend a Vietnam eVisa?

**Sometimes yes—but never treat it as automatic.** Extension eligibility depends on:

- Your **visa category** (tourist vs business vs other types)
- Your **nationality** and bilateral agreements
- Whether you have **already extended** during the same visit
- Your **immigration history** (prior overstays, violations, work without permits)
- **Local office policy** and current workload

**What extensions do not do:**

- They do not reopen the online eVisa portal for a top-up payment
- They do not change your **entry port** or convert **single entry to multiple entry**
- They do not authorize **employment** if you entered on a tourist authorization
- They do not forgive an **overstay** you already accumulated

Always read the conditions printed on your approval letter and the entry stamp in your passport. If your category explicitly prohibits extension, plan an exit instead.

## Can you extend a Vietnam eVisa online?

**Usually no.** The [official e-visa application channel](https://evisa.gov.vn/) is for **pre-arrival authorization**, not for lengthening a stay after you have entered. Extensions, when permitted, are handled through [Vietnamese immigration offices](https://immigration.gov.vn/) inside the country.

Searches for "extend Vietnam eVisa online" often surface:

- **Scam or agent websites** claiming instant online extensions
- **Confusion with visa exemption** rules (different from eVisa extensions)
- **Third-party "visa run" packages** that are exit-and-reapply services, not true extensions

**Official channel:** In-country immigration departments. **Private assistance (this site):** We help at **initial application** only—see [Vietnam eVisa fees explained](/blog/vietnam-evisa-fees-explained-2026) for upfront duration options.

If a website asks you to pay USD by card for an "online extension" without visiting an immigration office, treat it as high risk until you verify it with the local immigration department directly.

## Where Vietnam eVisa extensions are handled

Extensions are processed at **provincial-level immigration departments**—not at airports during departure (except in overstay/emergency situations you want to avoid).

Travelers commonly report filing in:

| City | Region | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hanoi** | North | High volume; arrive early; Vietnamese hotel confirmation letter helps |
| **Ho Chi Minh City** | South | District 1 area offices see heavy tourist traffic; English signage varies |
| **Da Nang** | Central | Popular with longer-stay visitors and expat-adjacent neighborhoods |
| **Other provincial capitals** | Nationwide | Possible if you are already outside major hubs—confirm the office handles tourist extensions |

**What to bring to the appointment (baseline checklist):**

| Document | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| **Original passport** | Must match eVisa data exactly |
| **Printed eVisa approval PDF** | Officers verify entry history against system records |
| **Passport photos (4×6 cm)** | Often required fresh; studio near immigration offices stock correct sizes |
| **Temporary residence declaration (Form NA7 / hotel registration)** | Hotels usually register you; Airbnb or informal stays complicate this |
| **Extension application (Form NA5 or current equivalent)** | Staff provide the latest version—do not rely on outdated PDFs from forums |
| **Proof of onward travel** | Flight or bus ticket showing intent to depart eventually |
| **Extension fee in VND** | Card payment is **rare** on-site—carry cash or confirm bank transfer rules |
| **Supporting letter (if applicable)** | Medical, family emergency, or employer letter for business categories |

Photocopy everything before you surrender your passport if the office retains it for processing. Hotels need passport copies for registration regardless.

**Planning advice:** Ask your hotel front desk for a **temporary residence confirmation** in Vietnamese before you visit immigration. Airbnb hosts vary in willingness to sign forms—factor this into extension planning if you are not in licensed accommodation.

## Step-by-step: typical in-country extension flow

Procedures change by city and policy updates. This is the **common pattern** travelers describe—not a guaranteed script for every office.

1. **Confirm you are still within authorized stay** — Apply before your exit date. Do not wait until the last day if the office requires passport retention for several days.
2. **Gather hotel registration and Form NA5** — Request help from accommodation; incomplete residence paperwork is a frequent delay.
3. **Visit the immigration department** — Some cities accept walk-ins; others prefer appointments during peak season. Arrive early with copies.
4. **Submit application and pay fee in VND** — Keep the receipt. Fees are set at the counter, not on our website.
5. **Leave passport (if required)** — Processing often takes **3–7 working days**; express same-day service exists in some offices when workload allows.
6. **Collect passport with extension stamp or approval slip** — Verify the **new exit date** and category (**tourist** vs **temporary residence**) before you leave the counter.
7. **Update hotel registration if your stay continues** — Mismatched registration dates cause problems at the next extension or at exit.

Ask explicitly: *Is this a tourist stay extension or a temporary residence extension?* Mixing categories creates exit complications and may affect future eVisa applications.

## How long can a Vietnam eVisa extension add?

**There is no universal answer.** Extra days depend on:

- The **officer's discretion** at the local office
- Your **stated reason** (tourism, family, medical—documentation helps)
- Whether you have **extended before** on the same visit
- **Nationality** and any applicable bilateral arrangements
- **Visa type** at entry (tourist extensions differ from business)

**Do not assume:**

- Another full **90 days** automatically
- The same extension length a friend received last month
- That a denied extension can be immediately reapplied with identical documents

**Business eVisa holders** may need employer letters, invitation letters, or partner company documentation. Tourist extensions rarely require employment proof—but working remotely for foreign clients while on a tourist stay is a legal gray area that extensions do not regularize.

## When a Vietnam eVisa extension is unlikely or refused

Officers may deny extensions if you:

- **Already extended once** on the same visit
- **Worked in Vietnam** without a work permit or proper business authorization
- Entered on a **visa type that does not allow extension** under current rules
- Have an **overstay mark** or unresolved immigration violation
- Cannot provide **valid temporary residence registration**
- Show **no credible onward travel** or reason for the extra days
- Used an **agent with forged documents**—this creates long-term entry bans

**Red flags that trigger scrutiny:**

- Frequent back-to-back extensions without exiting
- Mismatch between stated tourist purpose and local activity
- Passport stamps from prior overstays in Vietnam or the region

If refused, repeated DIY attempts without legal guidance rarely help. Consider [Vietnam eVisa vs embassy visa](/blog/vietnam-evisa-vs-embassy-visa-2026) for complex long stays.

## What happens if you overstay your Vietnam eVisa?

**Do not overstay—even one day.** Consequences escalate with duration and may include:

| Overstay severity | Possible consequences |
| --- | --- |
| **Short overstay (days)** | Daily fines, exit delays, stern warnings |
| **Medium overstay (weeks)** | Higher fines, potential detention, deportation |
| **Long overstay or repeat violations** | Entry bans, refused future eVisas, employer complications |

**If you realize you will miss your exit date:**

1. Contact the **nearest immigration office immediately**—not on departure day at the airport
2. Do not rely on unofficial agents promising to "fix" overstays
3. Keep records of any fine payment and official letters
4. Understand that prior overstays may surface in [Vietnam eVisa rejection reasons](/blog/vietnam-evisa-rejection-reasons-2026) on future applications

Overstaying is an **official immigration violation**, not a travel inconvenience you can negotiate at check-in.

## Alternatives when extension is denied or unavailable

### Exit Vietnam and apply for a new eVisa

If your **single-entry** eVisa is fully used or your stay cannot be extended:

- Exit to a neighboring country (Cambodia, Laos, Thailand, etc.)
- Apply for a **new eVisa** from abroad if your nationality allows online filing
- Re-enter only when the new PDF is approved—processing is not instant

**Multiple-entry holders** should re-enter on the **existing** authorization within the validity window instead of applying anew—see [Vietnam multiple-entry eVisa guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-multiple-entry-guide-2026).

### Embassy or consulate visa for complex stays

Long-term work, study, marriage-based residence, or repeated extensions may require an **embassy-issued visa** or residence card—not repeated tourist extensions. Compare paths in [Vietnam eVisa vs embassy visa](/blog/vietnam-evisa-vs-embassy-visa-2026).

### Adjust your itinerary

Sometimes the lowest-risk option is a planned exit on time—even if it disrupts bookings. Overstay fines and future entry bans cost more than a rescheduled flight.

Rejected extensions do **not** refund your original eVisa fees—see [/refund-policy](/refund-policy).

## Regional tips: Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Da Nang

### Hanoi

- Expect **morning queues** at major immigration offices
- Bring hotel letters in **Vietnamese**; English-only printouts slow processing
- Verify third-party agent credentials—unlicensed brokers are a known problem in the north

### Ho Chi Minh City

- Offices in central districts see heavy expat and tourist volume
- English communication is more common than in smaller cities—but forms are still Vietnamese
- Plan around **rainy season holidays** when appointment slots fill

### Da Nang

- Popular with travelers who extend once for central coast stays
- Book appointments ahead in **peak season** (summer, year-end holidays)
- Convenient for exits via Da Nang International Airport if extension fails

**Planning advice across all cities:** Start the extension process when you still have **at least 7–10 days** of authorized stay remaining— not 48 hours before expiry.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can you extend a Vietnam eVisa online?

Usually no. Extensions, when available, are handled at immigration offices inside Vietnam before your current stay expires—not through the original eVisa portal or private assistance websites.

### How long can a Vietnam eVisa extension add?

Extra days depend on visa type, nationality, and officer discretion. Do not assume another full 90 days—many tourist extensions add a shorter period.

### Where do I apply for a Vietnam eVisa extension?

Visit provincial immigration departments in cities such as Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, or Da Nang. Confirm current procedures and forms at the office where you will apply.

### How much does a Vietnam eVisa extension cost?

Fees are paid in VND at the immigration counter and change by policy. Recent traveler reports often cite roughly 1,000,000–3,500,000 VND for short tourist extensions—always confirm live fees on site.

### How long does extension processing take?

Often 3–7 working days; longer around Tet and peak season. Your passport may be retained during processing—plan accordingly.

### Can I extend a Vietnam eVisa twice?

Not guaranteed. Officers may refuse a second extension on the same visit, especially without strong supporting reasons or valid registration.

### What happens if I overstay my Vietnam eVisa?

Overstaying can lead to fines, deportation, and future entry bans. Contact immigration immediately if you will miss your exit date.

### Is it better to extend or apply for a longer eVisa before travel?

Applying for the correct **up to 90-day** duration and entry type at initial filing is safer than relying on in-country extension. Use [/apply](/apply) before you book non-refundable long stays.

### What if my extension is denied?

You may need to exit Vietnam and apply for a new eVisa abroad, pursue an embassy visa for complex stays, or adjust your itinerary before your authorized stay ends.

### Can a private visa service extend my eVisa?

No. Private assistance services—including this site—help with **pre-arrival applications** only. In-country extensions are between you and Vietnamese immigration authorities.
