## Who can apply for a Vietnam eVisa online?

Under Vietnam's current e-visa policy, citizens of **all countries and territories** may apply for an electronic visa for tourism or short business visits, subject to passport validity and accurate application data . That broad eligibility is why "apply for Vietnam eVisa online" is a practical default for many international travelers.

Exceptions and planning edge cases still matter:

- **Visa-exempt nationalities** (including some UK passport holders on short stays) may not need an eVisa for every trip—but exemption length, entry type, and purpose limits apply. If your stay exceeds exemption days or you need multiple entry, apply for an eVisa anyway.
- **Dual nationals** should apply with the passport they will present at immigration, not the "convenient" one.
- **Minors and infants** need their own authorization; do not assume they travel on a parent's approval.
- **Special travel documents** (refugee documents, temporary passports) may need extra review—confirm before paying.

For nationality-specific rules, use the [Vietnam eVisa requirements guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-requirements-guide-2026) alongside the eligibility checker.

## Before you start: document and planning checklist

Gather everything in one folder on your phone or laptop **before** you open the form. Stopping mid-application to hunt for a passport scan is how typos slip in.

| Item | Why it matters | Practical tip |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Passport bio-page scan** | Officers verify identity against the machine-readable zone (MRZ) | Colour, flat, all four corners visible, no fingers, no flash on MRZ lines |
| **Digital portrait photo** | Automated and human checks reject casual selfies | Plain light background, face forward, eyes open—see [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026) |
| **Arrival and departure dates** | Must fit your visa type's stay limit | Align with flight tickets; build buffer if processing is tight |
| **Email you check daily** | Approval and correction requests arrive electronically | Avoid shared inboxes you cannot access on travel day |
| **Payment card** | Pays mandatory government fee plus any service fee | Confirm international charges with your bank |
| **Itinerary clarity** | Determines single vs multiple entry | Side trips to Cambodia, Thailand, or Laos and return usually need multiple entry |

**Passport validity:** Aim for at least **six months** beyond your arrival date and at least one blank page for stamps. Renew an expiring passport before applying—your eVisa is linked to the passport number submitted.

Flying within **72 hours**? Read [24-hour Vietnam eVisa guidance](/faq/24-hour-vietnam-evisa) before you pay and contact support immediately if you choose assisted filing.

## Step 1: Verify eligibility and choose the right visa type

On [/apply](/apply), Step 1 begins with contact details and visa selection. Choose honestly and strategically.

### Tourism vs business

Select **tourism** for holidays, family visits, sightseeing, and cruises. Select **business** for meetings, conferences, factory visits, or short commercial trips without local employment. A tourist eVisa does **not** authorize paid work in Vietnam—remote work for Vietnamese clients, teaching, or local employment need proper permits.

### Single entry vs multiple entry

This is the most expensive mistake travelers make. Government fees on our portal:

| Visa type | Government fee (USD) | Best for |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| **Single entry (90 days)** | **$55** | One arrival period; no leave-and-return during the trip |
| **Multiple entry (90 days)** | **$80** | Side trips out of Vietnam and back within the validity window |

- **Single entry:** You enter once. Leaving Vietnam and returning usually requires a new visa unless you hold multiple entry.
- **Multiple entry:** Worth the extra $25 government fee if your route includes Bangkok, Siem Reap, Vientiane, or a cruise that disembarks and re-embarks in Vietnam.

Unsure? Compare totals on [/fees](/fees) and read the [Vietnam multiple-entry eVisa guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-multiple-entry-guide-2026) before checkout.

## Step 2: Contact details and travel dates

Enter your **email**, phone number, and lead traveler name on the contact section. Then set:

- **Arrival date** and **departure date** (must fit the maximum stay for your visa type)
- **Processing speed** (standard, Urgent, or Super Urgent when available)
- **Number of passengers** (up to 15 in one group submission on our flow)

**Entry port note:** Our form does **not** ask you to pick an entry port during application. The authorized port appears on your **approved eVisa PDF** after processing. Know your landing airport or border crossing anyway—you must verify it matches your ticket before you fly. Details: [Vietnam eVisa entry ports](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026).

Use an arrival date that leaves enough processing margin. Applying the same day you fly is how travelers end up at check-in without a PDF.

## Step 3: Passenger details—match your passport exactly

For each traveler, type details **exactly** as printed on the passport bio page:

- **Full name** (including middle names—do not abbreviate)
- **Passport number** (keep leading zeros; verify against MRZ line)
- **Date of birth** in the format shown on your passport (often DD/MM/YYYY—not US month/day habit)
- **Nationality** and **gender** as coded on the bio page

Use the passport you will **present at immigration**, not an old, cancelled, or soon-to-expire booklet.

**MRZ check:** Read the two machine-readable lines at the bottom of the bio page character by character. Passport number typos and date-of-birth errors are the top rejection triggers. See [common Vietnam eVisa mistakes](/blog/vietnam-evisa-common-mistakes-2026) for a full pre-pay checklist.

For children and infants, each child needs their own passenger row. Guardian details and infant photo rules are covered in [Vietnam eVisa for families and minors](/blog/vietnam-evisa-for-minors-families-2026).

## Step 5: Upload passport scan and portrait photo

Upload quality matters as much as form accuracy. Blurry files cause resubmission loops.

### Bio-page scan standards

- **Colour** image of the full bio page
- **Flat** surface—all four corners visible
- **No fingers**, shadows, or glare hotspots on MRZ lines
- **Readable text** at 100% zoom before you upload

### Portrait photo standards

- Plain **light background**, face forward, eyes open
- No sunglasses or non-religious headwear
- No beauty filters or heavy shadows
- Recent image that resembles you at immigration

If uploads fail, resize without blurring text. JPG format is standard. Full specs: [Vietnam eVisa photo requirements guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-photo-requirements-2026).

## Step 6: Track status, download approval, and verify every field

Processing commonly ranges **within five business days** for standard handling, depending on volume, holidays (especially **Tet**), and application quality. Watch email—and spam folders—for approval or correction requests.

When your eVisa arrives:

1. **Download the PDF** and save offline on your phone
2. **Print one paper copy** for airlines and immigration
3. **Verify passport number, name, and dates** against your physical passport
4. **Check entry type** (single vs multiple) against your itinerary
5. **Confirm the entry port** matches your ticket's first international landing in Vietnam

Do not book non-refundable hotels until you hold the approved PDF. Processing realities: [Vietnam eVisa processing time guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-processing-time-2026).

Keep your **reference number** and payment confirmation. If status seems stuck, contact support with those details—do not submit duplicate paid applications unless advised.

Applying for children or a tour group? Many flows accept **up to 15 travelers** in one submission with a shared contact email.

- Each traveler—including infants—needs **their own authorization**
- Use each child's **own passport** data, not a parent's name in place of the child
- Infant photos need a plain background; retake if the face is blurred

## After approval: prepare for arrival in Vietnam

Your online application is not finished when you pay—it is finished when immigration stamps your passport.

**At check-in:** Airlines may ask for your eVisa PDF. Carry the printed copy and offline PDF on your phone.

**At immigration:** Present the passport used in the application with the printed eVisa. Officers may ask about onward tickets or hotel bookings—have confirmation accessible.

**During your stay:** Your eVisa authorizes the stay length and entry type printed on the PDF. Overstaying or working on a tourist authorization carries legal risk. Extensions are limited—see [Vietnam eVisa extension guide](/blog/vietnam-evisa-extension-guide-2026) if your plans change.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does it take to apply for a Vietnam eVisa online?

Most travelers complete the form in **15–30 minutes** if passport scan and portrait photo are ready. Government processing after payment is separate—commonly within a few working days for standard handling, longer during peak holidays or if documents need correction. Completing the form is not the same as receiving approval.

### Can I apply for a Vietnam eVisa on my phone?

Yes. Use stable Wi‑Fi, upload clear JPG images, and zoom the passport MRZ before submitting. Desktop is easier for final review of every field against your passport bio page.

### What if I make a mistake after paying?

Corrections after payment are limited. Contact support with your reference number before submitting a duplicate paid application. Serious typos in name, passport number, or date of birth often require a new filing.

### Do children need separate Vietnam eVisa applications?

Yes. Each traveler normally needs their own authorization. Group flows can bundle up to 15 passengers in one submission—see [Vietnam eVisa for families and minors](/blog/vietnam-evisa-for-minors-families-2026).

### How much does it cost to apply for Vietnam eVisa online?

On our portal: government fee (**$55** single / **$80** multiple) + **$59.99** service fee per passenger + any optional urgent processing. Confirm your total at [/fees](/fees) before payment.

### When should I apply before my flight?

Apply **at least 7–14 days** before departure when possible. Do not book non-refundable flights or hotels until you hold the approved PDF. If you fly within 72 hours, read urgent guidance and contact support immediately.

### Can I enter Vietnam at any airport with an eVisa?

No. You must arrive at the **port of entry printed on your approval PDF**—immigration rules tie entry to the authorized port on your issued document. Major hubs include Tan Son Nhat (SGN), Noi Bai (HAN), and Da Nang (DAD)—but your specific approval governs, not a general list. See [Vietnam eVisa entry ports](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026).

### What documents do I need to apply online?

Passport valid six months beyond arrival, colour bio-page scan, compliant portrait photo, accurate travel dates, working email, and payment card. Match every field to the passport you will carry at immigration.

### Is the Vietnam eVisa the same as visa on arrival?

No. A Vietnam eVisa is approved **before** travel and linked to your passport electronically. Visa on arrival involves a separate approval letter and stamp fee at select airports. Most travelers planning ahead use eVisa or embassy channels—compare options in [Vietnam eVisa vs embassy visa](/blog/vietnam-evisa-vs-embassy-visa-2026).
