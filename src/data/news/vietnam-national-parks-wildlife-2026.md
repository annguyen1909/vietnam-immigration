Vietnam’s **national parks** protect karst caves, lowland rainforest, and marine reserves among Southeast Asia’s most biodiverse corners. Secure your [Vietnam eVisa](/apply) and [entry requirements for your nationality](/check-requirement) before non-refundable park permits or domestic flights—processing often takes **3 hours to 3 working days**.

## Phong Nha–Ke Bang: caves and primary forest

**Phong Nha–Ke Bang** (Quang Binh) anchors adventure itineraries.

### Trip types

| Experience | Fit level | VND note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Paradise Cave boardwalk | Families | ~150,000–300,000 entry band |
| Dark Cave zipline/swim | Moderate | Bundled tours vary |
| Tu Lan multi-day | Strong fitness | Millions VND; book licensed ops |
| Son Doong expeditions | Expedition screening | Premium pricing; long lead times |

**September–November** brings flood advisories—confirm river levels before Tu Lan swims.

## Cat Tien: primates near Ho Chi Minh City

**Cat Tien** lies a few hours from **Ho Chi Minh City**—practical for **weekend wildlife**.

- Dawn jeeps raise **gibbon** calls and **hornbill** flyovers
- Night walks spotlight insects and **civets** when rangers permit
- Stay near the park gate to cut road time

## Ba Be and the northeast highlands

**Ba Be National Park** centres on Vietnam’s largest natural lake, framed by limestone and **Tay**/**Dao** villages. Boat trips, short hikes, and homestays suit slower travel after busy **Hanoi** days. Winding roads from **Noi Bai** need buffer hours.

## Con Dao: marine turtles and diving

**Con Dao** blends penal-colony history with protected waters for **turtles**, reef fish, and seasonal diving. Flights from **Ho Chi Minh City** are usual access; respect **VND** marine fees and ranger guidance on nesting beaches (**no flash**).

## Cuc Phuong and Bach Ma: central corridors

**Cuc Phuong** (Ninh Binh region) offers primate rescue centres and mature trails from **Hanoi** or **Ninh Binh** day trips. **Bach Ma** above **Hue** delivers cooler ridge walks and waterfalls—pair with imperial sights on north–south routes.

## Planning tips for 2026

- **Permits & guides:** Never stray into restricted habitat; refuse illegal wildlife products
- **Health:** Repellent in lowlands; check malaria guidance only for remote border zones if applicable
- **Packing:** Rain shell, sturdy shoes, headlamp for caves/night walks
- **Visa timing:** Apply before park and flight deposits—[/faq/vietnam-evisa-requirements](/faq/vietnam-evisa-requirements) and [/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026](/blog/vietnam-evisa-approved-entry-ports-2026)
