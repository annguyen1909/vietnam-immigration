## Water puppetry (roi nuoc)

**Roi nuoc** began in **Red River Delta** villages where farmers entertained after harvest floods. Puppets glide on water behind a bamboo screen; musicians play **trong com** (rice drum), **dan bau** (monochord), and flute lines that bend like speech.

### Where to watch in 2026

| Venue | Location | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Thang Long Water Puppet Theatre** | Near Hoan Kiem, Hanoi | Most famous; multiple daily shows; English subtitles often |
| **Vietnam National Puppetry Theatre** | Hanoi (west of centre) | Larger stage; fewer tourists |
| **Museum of Ethnology** | Hanoi | Occasional outdoor shows—check schedule |
| **Village ponds** | Delta provinces | Festival-season only; needs local guide |

### What to expect

- Shows run **45–60 minutes**—folk tales, dragon dances, and agricultural humour
- Front rows may **splash slightly**; mid-rows balance splash and sightlines
- Tickets often **100,000–200,000 VND**—verify at box office; hotel markups are common
- Arrive **20 minutes early** for English surtitles and decent seats
- School holiday weeks (summer) sell out—book **1–2 days ahead**

## Ca tru: intimate chamber song

**Ca tru** features female vocalists, audience **phach** clappers, and the three-string **dan day** in call-and-response. Once associated with urban guild gatherings, it is now UNESCO-recognised heritage performed in restored **den** (guild houses).

### Booking and etiquette

- **Ca Tru Thang Long** and Old Quarter houses schedule **evening sets** (often 20:00)
- Photography may be **restricted**—phones on silent; no flash
- Tea or tasting menus bundled: **300,000–700,000 VND** depending on venue
- Dress **smart-casual**; rooms are hush-sensitive
- Tip only when jars or programmes permit—otherwise buy **CDs** or artisan tea

**Who it suits:** Travellers who enjoy **slow, formal** music—not arena spectacle. Skip if you need constant visual stimulation; the reward is vocal nuance.

## Quan ho, cheo, and tuong beyond Hanoi

Not all traditional arts live in the capital.

### Quan ho folk song (Bac Ninh)

**Quan ho** pairs male and female duets across river villages—silk costumes, boat stages, and call-and-response lyrics about love and separation. **Lim Festival** (lunar February) is the headline, but cultural houses host year-round programmes with notice.

Day trip from Hanoi: **1–1.5 hours** to Bac Ninh; combine with [Vietnamese festivals guide](/blog/vietnamese-festivals-cultural-celebrations-2026) for dates.

### Cheo and tuong theatre

**Cheo** is northern folk opera—humorous, accessible, village-rooted. **Tuong** (hat boi) is classical masked opera with elaborate costumes; harder to find with English context but visually stunning.

Check **Vietnam National Cheo Theatre** (Hanoi) schedules; tuong appears more in **Hue** and festival programmes.

### Nha nhac court music (Hue)

**Nha nhac** is imperial court music—drums, horns, and choreographed ritual. Performances run when scheduled at the **Hue Imperial City**; confirm Citadel event boards. Pair with tomb visits for a full imperial day.

## Contemporary art in Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi

**District 1** and **District 3** galleries in Saigon open late on weekends—many list **VND** prices beside works. Respectful browsing is welcome; ask before photos.

**Hanoi** creative clusters around **Hang Bong** and west-lake cafés host smaller shows. **Factory Contemporary Arts Centre** (HCMC) and **Manzi** (Hanoi) programme installations and talks.
