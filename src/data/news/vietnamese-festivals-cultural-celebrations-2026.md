## Tet Nguyen Dan (Lunar New Year) 2026

**Tet** is Vietnam's defining festival. Kitchens steam **banh chung** (square sticky rice cakes) and **banh tet** (cylindrical southern versions). **Peach blossoms** brighten northern homes; **apricot blossoms (hoa mai)** signal spring in the south.

**2026 timing:** Tet day falls on **17 February 2026**. Public holidays typically cluster **mid-February**—confirm the government's official window before locking international flights.

### Transport and closures

Workers return to family villages before **Tet Eve (30 Tết)**, tightening buses, trains, and domestic flights. After the first days, cities exhale—fireworks schedules vary by ward. Expect **museum, visa-support, and government desk closures** for several days; schedule applications and support contacts **before** you fly.

**Booking rule:** Reserve trains and domestic flights **3–6 weeks ahead** for Tet; last-minute seats disappear.

### What visitors can enjoy

- **Flower markets (cho hoa)** in **Hanoi** (Quang Ba, Hang Luoc), **Ho Chi Minh City**, and **Can Tho**
- Public fireworks viewing zones—follow police barriers; drone bans are common
- Temple visits with incense offerings—dress covered shoulders and knees
- Street photography of altars and blossom stalls—ask before close-ups of families

Learn **"chuc mung nam moi"** (happy new year) and **"an khang thinh vuong"** (health and prosperity)—elders appreciate concise blessings.

## Hue Festival and imperial heritage

On festival years, **Hue** stages royal-inspired music, **ao dai** parades, and **Perfume River** performances against the **Citadel** silhouette. The event is **periodic, not yearly**—confirm whether a full **Hue Festival 2026** edition runs before centring your trip on it.

Between mega-events, nightly programmes, **dragon boat** ambience, and tomb circuits still punctuate the former imperial capital.

### Sites and timing

- **Imperial City (Dai Noi)**—allow a full day; combined tickets bundle zones
- **Royal tombs**—**Khai Dinh**, **Tu Duc**, **Minh Mang** pair well with boat or scooter loops
- **Modest dress** for sacred halls; remove hats indoors

Licensed guides run **300,000–600,000 VND** half-days—worth it for court ritual context. Sunrise tomb visits beat afternoon heat shimmer on the river.

Combine Hue with [Vietnamese music, arts, and culture](/blog/vietnamese-music-arts-culture-2026) for evening performances and museum mornings.

## Mid-Autumn Festival (Tet Trung Thu)

**2026 timing:** Approximately **25 September 2026** (15th day, 8th lunar month).

Children parade with **lion dances** beneath carp- and star-shaped lanterns. **Hanoi's Old Quarter**, **Hoi An**, and **Ho Chi Minh City** districts sell mooncakes—sweet lotus paste for gifting, mixed-nut savoury wheels for sharing.

### Family-friendly tips

Plastic toy stalls crowd sidewalks—mind bags in delighted crowds. Some workshops demonstrate bamboo lantern frames if you stay past peak evening hours. Noise curfews tighten residential alleys after **21:00**—keep balcony music low.

**Mooncake note:** Versions vary—try small bakery samples before buying gift boxes. Hotel minibars are the wrong benchmark.

## Hung Kings Commemoration (Gio To Hung Vuong)

**2026 date:** **26 April 2026** (10th day, 3rd lunar month).

Spring pilgrimages honour legendary founders at **Phu Tho** shrine complexes north of Hanoi—incense smoke, palanquin processions, and quiet zones near altars. Day trips from **Hanoi** need **early starts** (depart before 06:00 on peak day). Combine with **My Lam** hot springs if routing north.

Expect heavy traffic on highways **QL2** and **CT05**—book return transport before you go.

## Perfume Pagoda (Huong Pagoda) pilgrimage season

From **January through March**, pilgrims boat and hike to **Perfume Pagoda** caves in **Huong Son** commune—a day trip from Hanoi that peaks weekends and Tet adjacency. Cable cars and rowboats queue for hours; start at dawn.

**Budget:** **300,000–600,000 VND** per person including boat, entry, and modest lunch—cash essential.

**Fitness:** The upper cave climb involves steep steps—skip if mobility is limited; lower temples still satisfy cultural interest.

## Lim Festival and quan ho folk song

Near **Bac Ninh** (under an hour from Hanoi), **Lim Festival** celebrates **quan ho** call-and-response folk singing between river villages. Dates shift on the lunar calendar—often **February**. Boat stages, silk costumes, and duet exchanges reward early arrival.

Less famous than Tet but **more manageable** for first-time festival travellers seeking living heritage without national transport gridlock.

## Vesak (Phat Dan) and pagoda luminaries

**Vesak** brightens pagoda courtyards with lotus candles and bodhisattva tableaux—typically **May** on the lunar calendar. Volunteer queues distribute vegetarian portions; confirm **VND** donations at labelled counters rather than informal touts.

Dress covered shoulders; remove shoes at thresholds; mute phones during chanting.

## Hoi An lantern nights (monthly)

On the **14th night of each lunar month**, Hoi An dims electric signs and floats lanterns on the Thu Bon River. Electric lanterns are sold to tourists for river release—follow local environmental guidance; some operators now collect lanterns after release.

**Cost:** Heritage stroll is often free; river lantern **10,000–30,000 VND**; heritage-ticket sites **120,000 VND** booklet for controlled entries.

Arrive **afternoon** for shophouse light; peak crowd **19:00–21:00**.

## Respectful participation checklist

- Queue calmly at pagoda gates; never skip security lines
- Ask before filming elders during household rituals
- Applause often suffices for street performers—tip only where jars or programmes allow
- Offer bus seats to parents with toddlers under lantern arches
- Carry **offline maps** when cellular networks choke in fireworks crowds
- Do not touch parade floats, lion-dance heads, or ritual objects without invitation
- Avoid red or white funeral-associated gift wrapping when visiting families at Tet
